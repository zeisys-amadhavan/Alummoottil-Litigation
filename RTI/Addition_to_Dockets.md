## STATEMENT OF FACTS

The deponent, **Anoop Madhavan, S/o Radhakrishnan Madhavan**, respectfully states as follows:

On 09.08.2024 at about 10:56 a.m. the deponent telephoned the Police Control Room (112) reporting that he was being attacked at Alummoottil Meda; the Station House Officer's RTI reply dated 15.12.2025 records the call. SI (Gr) Vishal V (PEN 170080) and Driver CPO 7029 Aneez reached the scene by about 11:15 a.m. (item 2(b)).

The same day the deponent was medically examined at the Govt. Taluk Hospital, Haripad and at Huda Trust Hospital, Haripad (2:39 p.m.), the latter recording a **right subscapularis tendon injury**.

The same day, at about 4:20 p.m., the deponent submitted a **written complaint** at Kareelakulangara P.S. naming **Sivadasan and Ramesh Chandran, who orchestrated the attack, and four then-unidentified assailants** — **Complaint No. 15281021-2024-5-00770** — received in the Station and entered in the Petition Register on 11.08.2024, the enquiry officer noted therein being SI (Gr) Vishal V (PEN 170080) (RTI reply 05.02.2026, items 5 and 11). He **re-submitted the same complaint via the Kerala Police portal (Thuna) on 23.08.2024, No. 15281021-2024-5-00825**.

On the Station's continued inaction the deponent escalated in writing — to the District Police Chief, Alappuzha on 02.09.2024 (petition u/s 173 BNSS) and to the Deputy Inspector General of Police on 17.09.2024 — whereupon, on 21.09.2024, the Station e-mailed the deponent that the complaint stood **"closed," without any investigation**. The deponent thereupon filed **CMP 2828/2024** before this Hon'ble Court on 23.09.2024, arraying six accused: **Sivadasan Madhavan and Ramesh Chandran Madhavan** (the trustee-uncles who orchestrated the attack), **Natarajan, Nandakumar, Arun B Unni and Stalin**.

Per the police's RTI replies dated 15.12.2025 and 05.02.2026, in the immediate response to the 09.08.2024 complaint the officers who attended the 112 call and the scene — SI (Gr) Vishal V and Dvr CPO 7029 Aneez — received, examined or prepared no official record, questioned no person and recorded no statement, and prepared no note or report on the complaint (15.12.2025 items 1(c)–2(e); 05.02.2026 item 7). The Station holds no General Diary entry and no 112 dispatch/arrival/closure log for the incident (items 1–2).

Per the RTI reply dated 05.02.2026 there is **no record of any attempt to contact the deponent between 09.08.2024 and 31.08.2024** (no GD entry, call register, dispatch register or e-mail — items 12–15).

No FIR was registered on the deponent's complaint until **17.12.2024 — Crime No. 956/2024 — 130 days after the occurrence**; that FIR records its source as the **"Home Ministry Office"** (CMO-portal petition No. G 2241200251) and **no reason for the delay** (item 8). In Crime No. 956/2024 the police arrayed only **Nadarajan (the Natarajan named in CMP 2828/2024), Nandakumar, Bobi and Stalin** — thereby **omitting the two orchestrators (Sivadasan and Ramesh Chandran) and Arun B Unni**, all of whom the deponent had named in CMP 2828/2024, and **adding one Bobi, whom the deponent never named** and who is shown in FIR 574/2024 as a co-worker of that case's complainant, Arun B Unni. The accused who were charged were not arrested and were served only notice under Section 35(3) BNSS (RTI reply 05.02.2026, item 7 with enclosures).

By contrast, SI (Gr) Vishal V (PEN 170080) registered FIR No. 574/2024 against the deponent within 58 minutes of an oral complaint by Arun B Unni on 10.08.2024 and signed it. Arun B Unni is neither a member of the Alummoottil family nor connected to the Trust; his FIR records a Kollam address, and he is a labourer engaged through a contractor of Sivadasan, falsely shown therein as "Alummoottil Meda's Work Supervisor."
