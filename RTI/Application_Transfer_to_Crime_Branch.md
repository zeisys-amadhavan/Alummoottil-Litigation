# APPLICATION FOR TRANSFER OF INVESTIGATION TO THE CRIME BRANCH

*To be filed in the proceedings arising from CMP 2828/2024 before the Hon'ble Judicial First Class Magistrate Court-I, Haripad. (Facts are set out in the accompanying Statement of Facts, relied upon.)*

The petitioner, **Anoop Madhavan, S/o Radhakrishnan Madhavan**, respectfully submits:

- The petitioner is the complainant in **CMP 2828/2024** arising from the assault upon him on 09.08.2024 at Alummoottil Meda. He apprehends that a fair and impartial investigation cannot be expected of the local police, on the following grounds.

(a)  **On the petitioner's own complaint** (Nos. 15281021-2024-5-00770 and 15281021-2024-5-00825) **no FIR was registered and the Station records no attempt to contact the petitioner**; instead, on 21.09.2024 the Station e-mailed the petitioner that his complaint stood "closed" without investigation. FIR No. 956/2024 was registered only on 17.12.2024 — **130 days later** — and only on a Home Ministry reference.

(b)  On the very next day after the incident, the same Station **registered FIR No. 574/2024 against the petitioner within 58 minutes** of an oral complaint, recorded and signed by **SI (Gr) Vishal V (PEN 170080)** — the same officer who had attended the petitioner's 112 call and conducted the enquiry on the petitioner's own petition **without registering any FIR**.

(c)  In CMP 2828/2024 the petitioner named six accused — **Sivadasan and Ramesh Chandra (who orchestrated the attack), Natarajan, Nandakumar, Arun B Unni and Stalin**. In Crime No. 956/2024 the police arrayed only **Nadarajan, Nandakumar, Bobi and Stalin** — thereby **omitting the two orchestrators and Arun B Unni** (the very person who had filed the counter-FIR against the petitioner), and **adding one Bobi, whom the petitioner never named** and who appears in **FIR 574/2024 as a co-worker of its complainant** — the same persons placed on opposite sides by the same Station.

(d)  In Crime No. 956/2024 the accused were **not arrested, were served only notice under Section 35(3) BNSS**, and the weapon was not recovered owing to the delay.

- The foregoing gives rise to a **reasonable apprehension of partiality and suppression**, such that the investigation ought not to remain with the local police.

## PRAYER

It is therefore prayed that this Hon'ble Court be pleased to **entrust / transfer the investigation arising from CMP 2828/2024 to the Crime Branch**, to **monitor the investigation under its supervision**, and to pass such further orders as it deems fit and proper in the interest of justice.
