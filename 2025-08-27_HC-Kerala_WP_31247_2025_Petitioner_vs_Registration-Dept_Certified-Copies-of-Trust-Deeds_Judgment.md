## J U D G M E N T

**Dated this the 27th day of August, 2025**

The petitioner filed an application in the online mode before the 3rd respondent/Sub Registrar seeking certified copies of three trust deeds. The same is evidenced by Ext.P2 receipt. However, the certified copies sought for were not issued, on the premise that the application is preferred in the online mode. Thereafter, it is the claim of the petitioner that he filed application directly, duly affixed with the stamp. Still, the same is not acted upon.

**2.** The learned Government Pleader would submit that the certified copies can be issued, if the petitioner or his agent directly approaches the 3rd respondent/Sub Registrar for receiving the same.

**3.** Having heard the learned counsel for the petitioner and the learned Government Pleader, this Court directs the 3rd respondent to consider the application filed online as one legal and proper and to issue the certified copies sought for by the petitioner within a period of two weeks from the date of receipt of a copy of this judgment. Petitioner will produce a copy of the judgment before the 3rd respondent, for compliance. Needless to say that, the petitioner or his agent will directly appear before the 3rd respondent for receiving the documents.

The Writ Petition (Civil) is disposed of as above.
