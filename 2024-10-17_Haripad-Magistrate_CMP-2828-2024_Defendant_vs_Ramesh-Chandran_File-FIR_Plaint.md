## COMPLAINT FILED U/S. 175 R/W 210(1)(C) OF BNSS, 2023 AGAINST THE ACCUSED ABOVE NAMED

1. The Complainant is presently residing in the United States of America, who was born and raised in Alummoottil Meda, an ancient tharavadu (traditional house) situated in Muttom along the Nangiarkulangara-Mavelikkara road in the Alappuzha district. Alummoottil Meda is associated with a historically significant Ezhava family from the kingdom of Travancore. The Complainant and the Complainant's sister were raised in Alummoottil Meda developing a strong emotional connection to the ancestral home.

2. It is humbly submitted that the Alummoottil Meda is now held under a Trust called Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust, which is formed through a Trust deed No. 2/07 dated 04-01-2007 wherein the Complainant's father serves as a trustee and the Complainant is a beneficiary of this trust as per the Deed of Amendment of Trust No. 66/IV/2022 dated 10-11-2022. The Complainant possesses the full authority to access and visit the property.

3. It is pertinent to submit that Accused Nos. 3, 4, and 6 are completely unknown individuals to the complainant. The complainant has never had any previous interactions, communications, or dealings with the aforementioned accused persons. There is no evidence or material on record to establish any prior association or connection between the complainant and Accused Nos. 3, 4, and 6. As for Accused No. 5, the only information the complainant possesses is that this individual is the one who has registered the FIR against the complainant. The complainant has no prior knowledge of Accused No. 5 beyond this fact.

4. The Accused Nos. 1 and 2 are the uncles of the Complainant and serve as trustees of the Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust. It is crucial to note that there is a significant history of discord between the Accused Nos. 1 and 2 and the Complainant, marked by ongoing disputes and a lack of cordiality. This adversarial relationship may be relevant in assessing the validity of the allegations and the overall proceedings concerning the trust. In recent years, Accused No. 2 has made it increasingly difficult for the Complainant and his sister to access the Meda and there has been a continuous pattern of denial of entry into the Meda even though the Complainant has the complete right to enter the property. The Trust Deed contains no provisions by which a trustee can prohibit a beneficiary from entering the trust properties. Accused Nos. 3 to 6 are a group of individuals who were allegedly arranged and appointed by Accused Nos. 1 and 2 to further their interests.

5. It is respectfully submitted before this Hon'ble Court that the Complainant, in his capacity as a beneficiary of the Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust, travelled from the United States specifically to visit the aforementioned property. However, the Complainant was unjustly restrained from entering the premises by Accused Nos. 3 to 6. This action constitutes a clear violation of the Complainant's rights as a beneficiary of the Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust. It is evident that the said actions appear to be based on instructions of Accused Nos. 1 and 2, as there is no other justifiable reason for restraining the Complainant from accessing the Meda. Specifically, the actions of Accused Nos. 3 to 6 seem to have been directed by Accused Nos. 1 and 2, who harbor personal objections to the Complainant's visit to the Alummoottil Meda. This interference, driven by personal animus rather than any legitimate reason, further underscores the wrongful nature of the Accused's actions.

6. The individuals referred to as Accused Nos. 3 through 6 employed criminal force, with the intention to cause the death of the Complainant upon directions of Accused Nos. 1 and 2. The accused Nos. 3 through 6 attacked the complainant with sticks. The Complainant alone was unable to resist the attack resulting in a serious shoulder injury to the Complainant. During the altercation, Accused Nos. 3 through 6 mentioned multiple times that their intention was to kill the Complainant and they would not hesitate from doing it. The Complainant was severely beaten with a stick and hit multiple times. This resulted in an acute full-thickness subscapularis tendon rupture with medical retraction, that has resulted in a permanent right arm disability and significant loss of mobility, strength and range of motion. Consequently, the Complainant has endured significant pain and has required extensive medical treatment as a result of this assault. This injury necessitated the cancellation of a scheduled business trip to Chennai. The Complainant is currently undergoing treatment in the United States of America, which includes medical consultations and MRI scans, due to the severity of the injury and the resulting disability. Accused No. 1 and 2 have threatened to kill the Petitioner multiple times and has stated on various occasions that the only reason the Petitioner is alive is because the Petitioner is the son of Mr. Radhakrishnan Madhavan.

7. The Accused clearly communicated their intention to cause the death of the Complainant and the Complainant survived only due to the private defence measures taken by him. The long standing animosity and personal venge has turned into a huge safety concern even causing risk to the life of the Complainant requiring immediate legal action.

8. It is important to note that, in addition to wrongfully restraining the Complainant, Accused No. 5 also filed a vexatious police complaint against the Complainant, citing offences under Sections 115(2), 118(1), 296(b), and 3(5) of BNS, 2023. This Complaint alleges that the Complainant attacked certain individuals. However, the entirety of this complaint is baseless and appears to be fabricated, lacking any substantive evidence. It is evident that this action is a direct result of a prior grudge held against the Complainant.

9. It is pertinent to note that the Complainant lodged a Complaint with the Kareelakulangara Police Station on 23-08-2024, under complaint number 15281021-2024-5-00825. Despite the submission of this complaint, no action has been taken by the authorities. On 02-09-2024, the Complainant submitted a formal Complaint via email to the District Police Chief of Alappuzha under Section 173 of the BNSS. Due to continued inaction, the Complainant formally lodged a complaint with the Deputy Inspector General of Police on 17-09-2024. On 21-09-2024, the Complainant received an email from the Kareelakulangara Police Station indicating that the complaint had been closed. This action constitutes a violation of the Complainant's rights, and it appears that the SHO of Kareelakulangara Police Station may be acting under the influence of the Accused, who is believed to have significant connections within the police department. Hence, the Complainant is constrained to approach this Honourable Court with a prayer to direct the Kareelakulangara police to register an FIR based on the above complaint and to conduct investigation into the matter, arrest the Accused, file final report against the Accused and render justice to the complainant.

10. It is respectfully submitted that the entire incident and alleged crime occurred at Alummoottil Meda, and the complaint was duly filed at the Kareelakulangara Police Station. Therefore, the jurisdiction for this matter falls within the purview of this Honourable Court.

11. It is humbly submitted before this Hon'ble Court that the offences committed by the Accused are punishable under Sections 115(2), 118(1), 117(3), 126(2), 3(5), 61(1), 109, 49, 116(e), 127(2) and 351(3) of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023.

Therefore, it is humbly prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to direct the SHO of Kareelakulangara police to register a FIR against the above named Accused and to conduct speedy investigation and to file a final report at the earliest.

All the facts stated above are true and correct.
