## Affidavit

I, **Anoop Radhakrishnan Madhavan**, aged 48 years, S/o Radhakrishnan Madhavan, residing at 1201 Connecticut Ave NW Ste 531, PMB # 722189, Washington DC, USA, represented by my duly constituted Agent holding Power of Attorney, AG Kishore, aged 71 years, S/o AK Gopalan, r/o 12 TV Road, Chetpet, Chennai, Tamil Nadu — 600031 do hereby sincerely and solemnly affirm and state as follows:

### 1. Competence to swear

I am the plaintiff in the above suit and I am conversant with the facts and hence competent to swear this affidavit.

### 2. Nature of the suit

The above suit is filed for a declaration that the Plaintiff is a Beneficiary under Trust Deed No. 2/IV/2007 of the Alummoottil Tharavad Trust and for consequential reliefs including a declaration that Amendment Deeds No. 66/IV/2022 and 17/IV/2024 are illegal and void. The suit further seeks directions to restore the Plaintiff's rights over the ancestral Meda property, to restrain the Defendants from committing further acts of exclusion and to compel the production of the Trust accounts. The suit is filed based on the Plaintiff's lawful rights as a lineal descendant and Co-Beneficiary under the original Trust Deed No. 2/IV/2007 dated 04/01/2007.

### 3. Trust formation and beneficiary class

The Alummoottil Tharavad Trust was constituted by Trust Deed No. 2/IV/2007, dated 04-01-2007, registered at the Sub-Registrar's Office, Cheppad. Page 3, paragraph 5 states that the Trust is established for the benefit of the *"descendants of Alummoottil Chellamma Channatty."*

### 4. Plaintiff's lineage and beneficiary status

Trust Deed Page 2, clause 4 records Radhakrishnan as Chellamma's fourth son and the second Trustee. The Plaintiff is the only son of Radhakrishnan and therefore Chellamma's grandson; accordingly, the Plaintiff falls within the beneficiary class under the Trust Deed and within Section 3 of the Indian Trusts Act, 1882.

### 5. Unauthorised 17/IV/2024 amendment

On 01-03-2024, Defendants 2 and 3 procured 17/IV/2024 without the consent of all beneficiaries, in violation of Section 11 of the Indian Trusts Act 1882, with the object and effect of altering / excluding the Plaintiff's beneficial interest.

### 6. O.S. 84/2024 filing

On 06-03-2024, the Plaintiff filed OS 84/2024 before this Court, specifically pleading that Defendants 2 and 3 were creating documents to alter his beneficiary status and seeking protection against obstruction of access to Alummoottil Meda, Nalukettu, and Nelpura.

### 7. The 09/08/2024 incident

Despite OS 84/2024 being *sub judice*, on 09-08-2024, third parties appointed by Defendants 2 and 3 obstructed the Plaintiff's ingress into the Alummoottil premises, publicly terming him a trespasser. Defendants 2 and 3 also instigated an outside enforcer, Arun B. Unni (who is neither trustee, author, beneficiary, nor family member), to set the criminal law in motion against the Plaintiff with a false allegation of assault on 09-08-2024.

### 8. O.S. 243/2024 filing

On 14-08-2024, OS 243/2024 was filed by Defendant 2, misrepresenting to this Court that the Plaintiff has *"no connection with the Trust"* and suppressing Trust Deed 2/IV/2007.

### 9. Scope of records sought — temporal range

The Alummoottil Tharavad Trust was constituted on 04 January 2007. All productions below are sought for each FY from **2006–07 up to 2024–25, and for FY 2025–26 YTD** (to the latest closed month), unless a more specific date is stated.

### 10. Statutory right to accounts

Till date, neither Defendant 2 (Managing Trustee) nor the other trustees have produced audited accounts to the Plaintiff, who is a legal beneficiary and is entitled, under Sections 19 and 57 of the Indian Trusts Act, 1882, to obtain and inspect the accounts of the Trust.

### 11. Pre-suit demand and refusal

The Plaintiff has, in writing, approached Defendant 2 demanding the accounts. On two separate occasions — **03-10-2024 and 28-10-2024** — Defendant 2 ignored the registered notices; on **05-12-2024**, Defendant 2 refused to accept a registered notice, indicating active obstruction in providing the accounts to the Plaintiff.

### 12. Prayer for production

Therefore, it is humbly prayed that this Hon'ble Court be pleased to direct the Defendants to preserve and produce the following within a period as determined by this Hon'ble Court.

#### (a) Audited financial statements

Certified copies of all audited financial statements (year-wise): auditor's report, balance sheet, income & expenditure, notes / schedules, management letters, and trustee resolutions approving the accounts, for **FY 2006–07 through FY 2024–25, and FY 2025–26 YTD** if available.

Certified copies of all banking & investment records (year-wise): a list of all trust bank accounts (account numbers and banks), and for each FY the bank statements / passbooks for every account (including FDs / investments / loan or OD accounts), FD / investment statements, and bank reconciliations if maintained.

#### (d) Tax filings and correspondence

Certified copies of all applicable tax filings & correspondence (year-wise): copies of income-tax returns / computations, TDS / GST returns (if applicable), acknowledgments, and any notices / replies relating to the trust's accounts.

#### (g) Resolutions authorising O.S. 243/2024

#### (h) Late-production bar and adverse inference
