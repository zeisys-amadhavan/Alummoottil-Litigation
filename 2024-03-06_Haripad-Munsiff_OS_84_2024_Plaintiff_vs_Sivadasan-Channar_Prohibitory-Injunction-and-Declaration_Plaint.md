## Statement of Facts

**1.** Plaintiff is the son of the 3rd defendant, as well as a member of Alummoottil Tharavadu, Mottom P.O., Harippad. Defendant Nos. 1, 2 and 4 are the brothers of the 3rd defendant and defendants 5 to 7 are the legal heirs of Mr. Ravindran M @ Ravindran Channar, who is the elder brother of defendants 1, 2 and 4. Said Ravindran Channar died on 27-08-2022.

**2.** Plaint scheduled properties belong to Alummoottil Tharavadu. Plaint A scheduled property belonged to one 'Narayani Channathi', who died in the year 1968. Said Narayani Channathi executed a Will in the year 1956, in favour of her daughter 'Chellamma Channathi', who is the grandmother of the plaintiff. Said Will was registered at Karthikappally S.R.O. as Will Deed Number 13/1956. Chellamma Channathi died intestate in the year 1983. After that, the properties are jointly managed by the legal heirs of Chellamma Channathi. As per the said Will Deed, the very old traditional buildings named "Meda, Nalukettu and Nelpura" would be maintained as ancestral assets, and all the members of the family are having free access to the said buildings. Said buildings are scheduled as B, C, and D in the plaint, for convenience.

**4.** After, when things are going like that, defendants 1 to 4 and Mr. Ravindran Channar — who is the husband of the 5th defendant as well as the father of the 6th and 7th defendants — jointly registered a trust deed, in favour of the A scheduled property, named as "ALUMMOOTTIL THARAVAD TRUST". The trust deed was registered at Karthikappally SRO as Trust Deed Number 2/2007. The properties in Resurvey number 210/10, 11, 27, 19, 20, 28, 11/29 of Cheppad village, are the subject matter of the Trust Deed. "Meda, Nalukettu and Nelpura" are not included in the Trust Deed. The defendants have no right to make any documents relating to the "Meda, Nalukettu and Nelpura", which are the B, C, and D scheduled properties.

**5.** After that, the defendants made several documents regarding the scheduled properties, without considering the words of the family members. Very recently, the defendants are planning to merge B, C, and D scheduled traditional buildings to the trust property, also planning to change the nature of the building to a heritage hotel, by playing unfair and illegal methods. They are making certain attempts to disturb the rights of the plaintiff over the B, C, and D scheduled buildings and trying to obstruct the free access of the plaintiff, with deliberate intention to disturb the peaceful access of the plaintiff. The plaintiff got reliable information regarding the intentions of the defendants.

**6.** There were some litigations between the defendants and some other family members regarding the partition of the family property. The decisions of those suits are not preventing the plaintiff from filing this suit. The plaintiff requested the defendants not to merge the traditional buildings to the Trust, but the defendants are not ready to do so.

**7.** The defendants are continuously obstructing the plaintiff from the free access of the B, C and D scheduled buildings. As part of their game, the defendants are planning to make some documents through SRO Cheppad, on 07-03-2023. The future interference of the plaintiff is not possible if the defendants continue their acts and make documents regarding the scheduled properties, without the knowledge of the plaintiff. A permanent prohibitory injunction against the defendants is needed to restrain them from making any documents to merge the B, C and D scheduled properties to the A scheduled TRUST property.

**8.** Plaintiff further submits that a declaration is also needed, with regard to the legal right of the plaintiff over the B, C, and D scheduled traditional buildings. The defendants are continuously obstructing and loudly disclosed on 23-12-2024, in a meeting of the family members, that they are going to make some documents and it will be registered on 07-03-2024, at SRO Cheppad. Hence the plaintiff is filing the suit for permanent prohibitory injunction, restraining defendants 1 to 6 from making any documents resulting to merge the B, C, and D scheduled properties to the A scheduled TRUST property.

**9. The cause of action** for the suit has arisen on 23-12-2023, when the defendants disclosed about their plan to make documents regarding the B, C, and D scheduled properties, and on 28-12-2023, when the plaintiff got reliable information regarding the registration of the documents, at Cheppad Village, which is within the jurisdiction of this Honourable Court.

**13. Court Fee and suit valuation** — The value of B scheduled 'Meda' is Rs. 60,000/- and value of C scheduled 'Nalukettu' is Rs. 60,000/-, and the value of 'Nelpura' is Rs. 30,000/-. So the total value of the suit is calculated as Rs. 1,50,000/- (One Lakh Fifty Thousand). For the relief of permanent prohibitory injunction, the value is calculated as Rs. 75,000/- under Section 27(a) of the Kerala Court Fees and Suit Valuation Act, so Rs. 5,900/- to be paid as Court Fee, but the plaintiff is paying Rs. 590/- as 1/10 of the said amount under Section 4A of the above Act.

**L.B.F:** Total value of the suit is Rs. 1,50,000/-. So LBF of Rs. 1,500/- is to be paid under Section 76(2) of the above Act, but the plaintiff is paying Rs. 150/- now, as 1/10th of the said amount.

3. To allow such other relief or pass such other orders as deemed fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case.
4. Allow the cost of the suit, and allow to realize the same from the assets of the defendants.
