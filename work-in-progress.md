## I. Nature of the petition (one paragraph)

This petition invokes the supervisory jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court to set aside an interlocutory order obtained by a false affidavit, the falsity of which now stands demonstrated by the Respondents' **own subsequent production** of the very documents earlier sworn to be unavailable. The order so procured defeated, in substance, the time-bound direction issued by this Hon'ble Court in OP(C) 3278/2025.

## III. The continuous fraud

*(Points 1–2 are the escape-proof, self-proving core and should lead. Points 3–6 are contradictions established by the Respondents' own documents, bearing directly on the truth of the 16.02.2026 affidavit. Point 7 is the concealment sequence. The "background going to intent" paragraph is colour only.)*

1. **Two irreconcilable sworn registration numbers — one is necessarily false.** A trust bears one registration number. In O.S. 243/2024 (filed 14.08.2024) R2 swore the Trust is **"a private family trust constituted as per the registered deed No. 17/IV/2024"** (cause title "Reg No. 17/IV/2024"; Para 1). In the Reply (16.02.2026, Para 3) and the 18.05.2026 IA (cause title) R2 swears the Trust was **"registered on 04-01-2007… at the Sub Registrar's Office, Cheppad"** (i.e. 2/IV/2007). Both statements are by the same deponent to the same Court; one is false on its face, with no "period" or "drafting" defence. Pleading that the Trust was *"constituted as per"* the 2024 amendment — a deed dated 01.03.2024 — was the device by which the founding Deed 2/IV/2007, and the Petitioner's beneficiary status under it, was suppressed.

2. **Sworn unavailable, then produced.** On 16.02.2026 R2 swore the documents demanded in IA 5/2025 were **"not available for production before the court"** (Reply Para 13). On 18.05.2026 the Respondents produced them. The representation that closed IA 5/2025 was untrue, as the Respondents' own production proves.

3. **The "not available" sweep covered FY 2025–26 — and those very documents were produced.** IA 5/2025 expressly sought production for **"FY 2025–26 YTD"** (affidavit Para 9; prayers (a) and (c)). Reply Para 13 swept "the documents demanded in IA 5/2025" into a single "not available" claim, yet the affidavit's only factual basis (Paras 4–6) was confined to **FY 2007–2024**, while Para 8 itself disclosed a Federal Bank account opened in **May 2025** (within FY 2025–26). On 18.05.2026 R2 produced FY 2025–26 documents — bank statement through 15.05.2026, lease of 27.10.2025, minutes book. The "not available" claim was a misrepresentation as to the current-year documents when made, defeating any "dormancy was only 2007–24" defence.

4. **The minutes book existed in February and was withheld.** R2 produced only five selected resolutions on 16.02.2026 ("5 in numbers," Reply Para 9) and swore the rest "not available" (Para 13). On 18.05.2026 he produced the **Original Minutes Book**, which by definition contains the complete record of trustee meetings. If producible in May, it was in R2's possession and producible in February. The affidavit was false on this point.

5. **The lease pre-dated the affidavit by 112 days.** R2 executed the Sree Moolavasam lease himself on **27.10.2025** — 112 days before the 16.02.2026 affidavit — so it was necessarily in his possession when he swore the Trust **"operated as a dormant entity or held no assets/liabilities"** (Reply Para 6). A registered commercial lease of Trust property is the antithesis of dormancy. The affidavit was false on this point.

6. **Sworn dormant, while running construction with employees and holding property.** For FY 2007–2024 R2 swore the Trust had **"not received any funding, donations, income, or generated any revenue from any source whatsoever"** (Reply Para 4) and **"operated as a dormant entity"** (Para 6). Yet in O.S. 243/2024 he pleaded the Trust ran construction **"at the instance of plaintiff trust"** with **"3 employees deployed by the plaintiff trust"** (Paras 1, 4), and that the property is in the Trust's **"absolute possession and enjoyment"** via three settlement deeds (2022 and 2024). A trust with zero revenue does not deploy paid employees; and the Reply's own Para 6 carve-out ("except Settlement of Land and buildings to the trust") concedes the Trust held assets. Both sets of statements are R2's sworn words to the same Court; the dormancy claim is false.

7. **Concealed until confronted.** The lease was never disclosed until after the Petitioner named Sree Moolavasam on record (13.03.2026); only on 18.05.2026, after IA 6/2026, did the Respondents produce it — disclosure compelled, not volunteered. *(A further internal contradiction: R2 swears the account was opened "26/05/2025" in Reply Para 8 but "28/05/2025" in the 18.05.2026 IA — a third inconsistent sworn date.)*

### Background going to intent (pattern, pleaded as colour — not as a ground for relief)

The conduct before this Hon'ble Court is not isolated but the latest in a documented course of conduct, pleaded here only to show that it is **deliberate and continuous** (each item being a matter for adjudication in its proper forum, not a ground of relief in this petition):

(i) **Renovation was a personal project, not the Trust's.** R2 sued in O.S. 243/2024 on the basis that works were "at the instance of plaintiff trust" with trust "employees," yet the Reply (Para 7) reproduces a resolution that Sivadasan would **"carry out the renovation/rebuilding/construction… by spending all the funds from his personal accounts"** and is **"under no obligation to provide the details of any expenditure… to the trust or trustees or any other persons whatsoever"** — so the trust-agency basis of O.S. 243/2024 was constructed on a representation contradicted by R2's own later oath, and Trust-property works were deliberately structured to be unaccountable (raising foreign-remittance/blending concerns, Sivadasan being a US-resident OCI holder ⚠).

(ii) **No record supports the 09.08.2024 "employees" or the litigation against the Petitioner.** The Trust's first bank account opened **26.05.2025 — c. 290 days after 09.08.2024** (Reply Para 8); R2 admits no books and no income for the period. Across the 16.02.2026 and 18.05.2026 productions, no document evidences any wage paid to any person on/before 09.08.2024, any resolution employing staff, or any resolution authorising Arun B. Unni to act as security/enforcer, to lodge FIR 574/2024, or R2 to file O.S. 243/2024 on the Trust's behalf. *(Matter for O.S. 214/2025 and the Order VII Rule 11 attack on O.S. 243/2024.)*

(iii) **No resolution authorises the Sree Moolavasam arrangement** — neither incorporation of the company, nor the lease, nor commercial operations on Trust premises (⚠). Either undisclosed resolutions exist *(to be compelled in IA 6/2026)* or the entire arrangement is *ultra vires* the Deed.

(iv) **"No connection" pleaded despite admitting lineage.** O.S. 243/2024 Para 4 states the Petitioner **"is the son of Mr. Radhakrishnan.M"** yet "doesn't have any connection with the plaintiff trust… not a member" — contradicting both the Deed's beneficiary definition (descendants of Chellamma Channatty) and R2's own admission of lineage in the same paragraph; the Reply (Para 11) repeats "the plaintiff is not a beneficiary." *(Merits of O.S. 214/2025.)*

(v) **False cause of action in O.S. 243/2024** (event pleaded 13.08.2024) contradicted by the Petitioner's departure from India on 11.08.2024 per immigration record ⚠. *(Order VII Rule 11 attack.)*

(vi) **Non-consensual amendments** 66/IV/2022 and 17/IV/2024, procured without beneficiary consent ⚠. *(Merits of O.S. 214/2025.)*

(vii) **Wrong-jurisdiction registration.** The Trust property and registered office lie in **Cheppad, Alappuzha** (R2's own consistent description), yet the lease was registered at **Maradu Sub-Registrar's Office, which is in Ernakulam District** — and R2's 18.05.2026 IA wrongly describes that office as being in "Alappuzha." Under Section 28 of the Registration Act, 1908, a document affecting immovable property must be registered where the property is situated; registration by a Sub-Registrar lacking territorial jurisdiction renders the registration and the lease's admissibility (Section 49) **seriously questionable** ⚠. Coupled with this Hon'ble Court's earlier direction to **SRO Cheppad** to furnish the Petitioner a certified copy of the Deed, registering away from Cheppad raises a serious inference of evasion.

(viii) **Account-keeping was mandatory and admittedly omitted.** Clauses 2 and 16 of the Deed require the Managing Trustee to maintain accounts and present them annually; R2 admits none were maintained for FY 2007–2024 and identifies no resolution dispensing with the duty ⚠. *(Merits of O.S. 214/2025.)* **[⚠ HOLD pending verification: a related allegation — that Author 1, Dr. Madhavan Ravindran, died on 27.08.2022, rendering impossible any post-2022 resolution purportedly signed by all five Authors — must NOT be pleaded unless the death certificate AND the face/date/signatures of the resolution are confirmed. An unproven forgery allegation that misfires will damage the petition. Verify before including.]**

## IV. Grounds (short)

a. An order procured by a false affidavit is vitiated by fraud and is liable to be set aside; fraud unravels all (*S.P. Chengalvaraya Naidu*; *A.V. Papayya Sastry*).

b. The 90-day direction in OP(C) 3278/2025 was complied with in **form** but defeated in **substance**: a bare "closed in light of the affidavit" disposal, obtained by misrepresentation, is not the genuine disposal-on-merits the direction contemplated.

c. The alternative remedy is illusory: the Petitioner did approach the trial court (rejoinder; IA 5/2026; IA 6/2026), yet no effective adjudication has followed ⚠ *(insert docket dates / postings)*, and the trial court's order imposed no consequence despite the contradiction.

d. The falsity is **apparent on the face of the record** — established by the Respondents' own documents — and requires no trial, warranting supervisory intervention.

## V. Prayer

It is therefore most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

**a. (Primary)** Set aside the order dated 04.03.2026 closing IA No. 5/2025 in O.S. No. 214/2025 as having been procured by a false affidavit, and direct genuine reconsideration of the production/discovery applications with discovery on oath;

**b. (Alternative, without setting aside)** Direct the trial court to dispose of IA No. 6/2026 and the pending applications **expeditiously, within a fixed time, by a reasoned order on merits**;

**c. (In any event)** Record the contradiction between the affidavit dated 16.02.2026 and the production dated 18.05.2026, and direct the trial court to take the said contradiction and the 18.05.2026 production into account in deciding the applications;

**d.** Grant liberty to the Petitioner to pursue appropriate proceedings (including under Section 340 CrPC / Section 379 BNSS and for recall) in respect of the false affidavit, and leave it open to this Hon'ble Court to take such action as it deems fit in respect thereof;

**e.** Direct maintenance of **status quo** in respect of the Trust property and the commercial venture pending disposal of the applications;

**f.** Costs, and such other relief as this Hon'ble Court deems fit.
