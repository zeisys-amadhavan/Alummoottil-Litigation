## I. Nature of the petition (one paragraph)

This petition invokes the supervisory jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court because the **Respondents' false affidavit dated 16.02.2026 prevented the genuine adjudication that this Hon'ble Court had directed** in OP(C) 3278/2025. The Respondents swore that the documents sought were **"not available for production,"** and the trial court — acting in good faith on a sworn statement it was entitled to accept — proceeded on that footing. The Respondents have now, on 18.05.2026, **produced the very documents they had sworn were unavailable**, demonstrating that the affidavit was false. *(The record of 04.03.2026 is capable of more than one reading as to the status of IA 5/2025 — see Ground (b); the Petitioner seeks clarification, and relief in the alternative.)* The Petitioner respectfully seeks that this Hon'ble Court **assist the trial court** by directing that the pending applications be taken up and decided on merits with genuine discovery, that the contradiction be recorded, and that the Respondents' conduct across the connected suits pending before the same Court be had regard to on the question of credibility.

## III. The continuous fraud

*(Points 1–2 are the escape-proof, self-proving core and should lead. Points 3–6 are contradictions established by the Respondents' own documents, bearing directly on the truth of the 16.02.2026 affidavit. Point 7 is the concealment sequence. The "background going to intent" paragraph is colour only.)*

1. **Two irreconcilable sworn registration numbers — one is necessarily false.** A trust bears one registration number. In O.S. 243/2024 (filed 14.08.2024) R2 swore the Trust is **"a private family trust constituted as per the registered deed No. 17/IV/2024"** (cause title "Reg No. 17/IV/2024"; Para 1). In the Reply (16.02.2026, Para 3) and the 18.05.2026 IA (cause title) R2 swears the Trust was **"registered on 04-01-2007… at the Sub Registrar's Office, Cheppad"** (i.e. 2/IV/2007). Both statements are by the same deponent to the same Court; one is false on its face, with no "period" or "drafting" defence. Pleading that the Trust was *"constituted as per"* the 2024 amendment — a deed dated 01.03.2024 — was the device by which the founding Deed 2/IV/2007, and the Petitioner's beneficiary status under it, was suppressed.

2. **Sworn unavailable, then produced.** On 16.02.2026 R2 swore the documents demanded in IA 5/2025 were **"not available for production before the court"** (Reply Para 13). On that footing the pleadings were closed (04.03.2026). On 18.05.2026 the Respondents produced those very documents. The sworn basis on which the Respondents resisted production — and on which the IAs then stalled — was untrue, as their own production proves.

3. **The "not available" sweep covered FY 2025–26 — and those very documents were produced.** IA 5/2025 expressly sought production for **"FY 2025–26 YTD"** (affidavit Para 9; prayers (a) and (c)). Reply Para 13 swept "the documents demanded in IA 5/2025" into a single "not available" claim, yet the affidavit's only factual basis (Paras 4–6) was confined to **FY 2007–2024**, while Para 8 itself disclosed a Federal Bank account opened in **May 2025** (within FY 2025–26). On 18.05.2026 R2 produced FY 2025–26 documents — bank statement through 15.05.2026, lease of 27.10.2025, minutes book. The "not available" claim was a misrepresentation as to the current-year documents when made, defeating any "dormancy was only 2007–24" defence.

4. **The minutes book existed in February and was withheld.** R2 produced only five selected resolutions on 16.02.2026 ("5 in numbers," Reply Para 9) and swore the rest "not available" (Para 13). On 18.05.2026 he produced the **Original Minutes Book**, which by definition contains the complete record of trustee meetings. If producible in May, it was in R2's possession and producible in February. The affidavit was false on this point.

5. **The lease pre-dated the affidavit by 112 days.** R2 executed the Sree Moolavasam lease himself on **27.10.2025** — 112 days before the 16.02.2026 affidavit — so it was necessarily in his possession when he swore the Trust **"operated as a dormant entity or held no assets/liabilities"** (Reply Para 6). A registered commercial lease of Trust property is the antithesis of dormancy. The affidavit was false on this point.

6. **Sworn dormant, while running construction with employees and holding property.** For FY 2007–2024 R2 swore the Trust had **"not received any funding, donations, income, or generated any revenue from any source whatsoever"** (Reply Para 4) and **"operated as a dormant entity"** (Para 6). Yet in O.S. 243/2024 he pleaded the Trust ran construction **"at the instance of plaintiff trust"** with **"3 employees deployed by the plaintiff trust"** (Paras 1, 4); the Trust held substantial immovable property under **three registered settlement deeds (1669/2022, 298/2024, 299/2024)** transferred to it; and the Reply's own Para 6 carve-out ("except Settlement of Land and buildings to the trust") concedes assets. A trust with zero revenue does not deploy paid employees; a trust holding settled property is not without assets. Both sets of statements are R2's sworn words to the same Court; the dormancy claim is false.

7. **Concealed until confronted.** The lease was never disclosed until after the Petitioner named Sree Moolavasam on record (13.03.2026); only on 18.05.2026, after IA 6/2026, did the Respondents produce it — disclosure compelled, not volunteered. *(A further internal contradiction: R2 swears the account was opened "26/05/2025" in Reply Para 8 but "28/05/2025" in the 18.05.2026 IA — a third inconsistent sworn date.)*

### Background going to intent (pattern, pleaded as colour — not as a ground for relief)

The conduct before this Hon'ble Court is not isolated but the latest in a documented course of conduct, pleaded here only to show that it is **deliberate and continuous** (each item being a matter for adjudication in its proper forum, not a ground of relief in this petition):

(i) **Renovation was a personal project, not the Trust's.** R2 sued in O.S. 243/2024 on the basis that works were "at the instance of plaintiff trust" with trust "employees," yet the Reply (Para 7) reproduces a resolution that Sivadasan would **"carry out the renovation/rebuilding/construction… by spending all the funds from his personal accounts"** and is **"under no obligation to provide the details of any expenditure… to the trust or trustees or any other persons whatsoever"** — so the trust-agency basis of O.S. 243/2024 was constructed on a representation contradicted by R2's own later oath, and Trust-property works were deliberately structured to be unaccountable (raising foreign-remittance/blending concerns, Sivadasan being a **US citizen holding an OCI card**).

(ii) **No record supports the 09.08.2024 "employees" or the litigation against the Petitioner.** The Trust's first bank account opened **26.05.2025 — c. 290 days after 09.08.2024** (Reply Para 8); R2 admits no books and no income for the period. Across the 16.02.2026 and 18.05.2026 productions, no document evidences any wage paid to any person on/before 09.08.2024, any resolution employing staff, or any resolution authorising Arun B. Unni to act as security/enforcer, to lodge FIR 574/2024, or R2 to file O.S. 243/2024 on the Trust's behalf. *(Matter for O.S. 214/2025 and the Order VII Rule 11 attack on O.S. 243/2024.)*

(iii) **A commercial enterprise was operating on Trust premises while the Trust was sworn "dormant."** Sree Moolavasam (GSTIN 32ABQCS9416G1Z1) was GST-registered on 09.12.2025 for "residential health-care services," with its principal place of business at the Trust premises ("2/242, Alumoottil, Muttom, Haripad"), and filed **monthly GST returns for Oct–Dec 2025 and Jan–Mar 2026** — i.e. it was actively transacting on Trust property across the same period covered by the dormancy posture. No resolution authorising the arrangement (incorporation, lease, or operations) has been disclosed; either undisclosed resolutions exist *(to be compelled in IA 6/2026)* or the arrangement is *ultra vires* the Deed. *(Plead the company's GST activity as proof of activity on Trust premises; the lease income question is for discovery.)*

(iv) **"No connection" pleaded despite the lineage on record.** O.S. 243/2024 Para 4 states the Petitioner **"is the son of Mr. Radhakrishnan.M"** yet "doesn't have any connection with the plaintiff trust… not a member"; the Reply (Para 11) repeats "the plaintiff is not a beneficiary." This contradicts the beneficiary class in the 2007 Deed via a two-step chain on the record: the Respondents admit the Petitioner is Radhakrishnan's son, and the Deed itself names the authors (including Radhakrishnan) as sons of **Chellamma Channatty** — so the Petitioner is her descendant and within the class. *(Plead it as their admission + the Deed, NOT as their admission that he is "Chellamma's grandson," which they did not concede. Merits of O.S. 214/2025.)*

(v) **False cause of action in O.S. 243/2024.** That plaint pleads the cause of action arose on **13.08.2024**, being "the date on which the plaintiff has got reliable information that the defendant is making hasty preparations to trespass" — yet the Petitioner's **immigration exit stamp is dated 10.08.2024**, he departed India on 11.08.2024, and has not returned. A cause of action premised on the Petitioner's imminent physical trespass on 13.08.2024 is contradicted by the public immigration record. *(Order VII Rule 11 attack; cf. Dahiben.)*

(vi) **Non-consensual amendments, and a deceased author's wife inserted to make up the signatories.** The 2007 Deed named five authors, including Author No. 1 Dr. Madhavan Ravindran Channar. The Respondents' own Amendment 66/IV/2022 records that he **died on 27.08.2022**. Amendment **17/IV/2024** was nonetheless executed by five "trustees," the fifth being **C.K. Geeta, the wife of the late Ravindran** — yet no instrument has been shown admitting her as a trustee under clause 15 of the Deed. The amendments altering the *beneficiary definition* were procured without the consent of all beneficiaries (Section 11 ITA) and, on the face of the Respondents' own documents, by substituting a deceased author's wife to constitute the required signatories. ⚠ *(Plead these as facts from the Respondents' own deeds; do NOT assert "forgery" as a conclusion. **Anticipate two defence footholds in the Deed:** clause 14 lets the authors jointly nominate/change trustees, and clause 15 says legal heirs of a deceased author/trustee "shall be admitted" — so the defence will argue (a) the authors had amendment power and (b) Geeta's admission was deed-sanctioned. The Petitioner's answer: clause 11/changing-the-beneficiary-class is distinct from changing trustees; clause 15 admission still requires a shown process/instrument, none produced; and a deceased author cannot himself consent. Have this answer ready — the Section 11 "all beneficiaries must consent" point is NOT self-evident on this Deed. Merits of O.S. 214/2025.)*

(vii) **Wrong-jurisdiction registration, against the backdrop of a prior HC direction to SRO Cheppad.** The Trust property and registered office lie in **Cheppad, Alappuzha** (R2's own consistent description), yet R2's 18.05.2026 IA admits the Sree Moolavasam lease was *"registered through the Marad, Alappuzha Sub-Registrar's Office"* — Marad/Maradu being in **Ernakulam District**, not Alappuzha, and not SRO Cheppad. Under **Section 28 of the Registration Act, 1908**, a document affecting immovable property must be registered where the property is situated; registration by a Sub-Registrar lacking territorial jurisdiction renders the registration and the lease's admissibility (Section 49) seriously questionable. Critically, by judgment dated **27.08.2025 in WP(C) 31247/2025 (2025:KER:65679)** this Hon'ble Court had directed **SRO Cheppad** to issue the Petitioner certified copies of the trust deeds — so SRO Cheppad was, to the Respondents' knowledge, the office under this Court's scrutiny re the Trust's documents. The lease was registered at Marad **two months later (27.10.2025)**, over property indisputably within SRO Cheppad's jurisdiction. The Petitioner submits this sequence raises a serious inference that registration was shifted away from Cheppad, and **seeks that notice issue to the Marad Sub-Registrar to explain the registration of a lease over property outside its territorial jurisdiction.** ⚠ *(Plead the timeline and the prior direction as facts; leave motive as the inference they compel.)*

(viii) **Account-keeping was mandatory and admittedly omitted, despite pre-suit demand.** Clauses 2 and 16 of the Deed require the Managing Trustee to maintain accounts and present them annually; R2 admits none were maintained for FY 2007–2024 and identifies no resolution dispensing with the duty. The Petitioner had demanded accounts pre-suit by registered post on 03.10.2024, 28.10.2024 and 05.12.2024 (postal receipts held), which were ignored/refused. *(Merits of O.S. 214/2025; supports the beneficiary's right to accounts under ss. 19 & 57 ITA.)*

(ix) **Impossibility on the face of the record (related to (vi)).** Given Author No. 1's death on 27.08.2022 (recited in the Respondents' own Amendment 66/IV/2022), to the extent any instrument relied upon by the Respondents purports to carry the concurrence or signature of all five original Authors after that date, it is an impossibility requiring explanation. ⚠ *(Plead the death as fact from 66/IV/2022; frame strictly conditionally — do NOT assert forgery until a specific post-August-2022 instrument bearing his assent/signature is identified.)*

## IV. Grounds (short)

a. A litigant who secures any advantage — here, resisting production by swearing the documents were "not available" — on a **false affidavit** commits fraud on the court; fraud unravels all, and the advantage cannot stand (*S.P. Chengalvaraya Naidu*; *A.V. Papayya Sastry*). The trial court was **entitled to act on the sworn statement and did so in good faith**; the falsity is now established by the Respondents' own 18.05.2026 production. It is the Respondents' deception, not any act of the trial court, that is impugned.

b. **In the alternative, as to the status of IA 5/2025:** the order-sheet of 04.03.2026 records *"Petition closed in light of the affidavit. For hearing IAs,"* while the register reflects IA 5 as disposed. The Petitioner seeks clarification of this record. *To the extent* IA 5 stands disposed, it was disposed without a reasoned order and upon an affidavit now shown to be false, and is liable to be set aside; *to the extent* it remains pending, the genuine disposal this Hon'ble Court directed has been **prevented by the Respondents' false affidavit** and remains to be done.

c. The three-month direction in OP(C) 3278/2025 (15.01.2026, 2026:KER:3035) could not achieve its purpose **through no fault of the trial court**: the court acted promptly (advancing the matter *suo motu* on 06.02.2026 upon receipt of the order), and the period elapsed during ordinary court exigencies — the Munsiff attending a conference at the Hon'ble Supreme Court (09.03.2026), a declared holiday (09.04.2026), and the court vacation (to 18.05.2026). The Respondents' false affidavit, not any judicial delay, is what stood in the way of a genuine adjudication.

d. The falsity is **apparent on the face of the record** — established by the Respondents' own documents — and requires no trial, warranting supervisory intervention.

e. The same false posture runs across the Respondents' connected litigation before the **same Court** — O.S. 84/2024, O.S. 243/2024 and O.S. 214/2025, all involving the same parties, the same Trust and the same property — demonstrating that the 16.02.2026 misstatement is part of a **deliberate and continuous course of conduct**, not an isolated error; the connected matters are properly considered as a whole. ⚠ *(confirm all three are currently pending before the Munsiff Court, Haripad.)*
