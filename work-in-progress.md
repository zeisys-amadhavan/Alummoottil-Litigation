## I. Nature of the petition (one paragraph)

This petition invokes the supervisory jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court to set aside an interlocutory order obtained by a false affidavit, the falsity of which now stands demonstrated by the Respondents' **own subsequent production** of the very documents earlier sworn to be unavailable. The order so procured defeated, in substance, the time-bound direction issued by this Hon'ble Court in OP(C) 3278/2025.

## III. The continuous fraud (the core — three self-proving points)

1. **Sworn unavailable, then produced.** On 16.02.2026 the Respondents swore the documents sought in IA 5/2025 were "not available for production" (⚠ Para 13). On 18.05.2026 they produced those documents. The representation that closed the application was untrue.

2. **Sworn dormant, while operating.** On 16.02.2026 they swore dormancy and "no books." Their own O.S. 243/2024 pleads deployed "employees" and construction (⚠ Para 5); and a commercial lease executed **27.10.2025 — nearly four months before the affidavit** — already existed. Dormancy was false when sworn.

3. **Concealed until confronted.** The lease was never disclosed until after the Petitioner named Sree Moolavasam on record (13.03.2026); only on 18.05.2026, after IA 6/2026, did the Respondents produce it — a pattern of disclosure compelled, not volunteered.

*(Pattern / corroboration, pleaded briefly and not as the basis of relief: suppression of the founding Deed in O.S. 243/2024; unilateral amendments 66/IV/2022 & 17/IV/2024 without beneficiary consent; registration of the lease away from SRO Cheppad — the very office this Hon'ble Court had earlier directed to furnish the Petitioner a certified copy of the Deed — raising a serious inference of evasion ⚠.)*

## IV. Grounds (short)

a. An order procured by a false affidavit is vitiated by fraud and is liable to be set aside; fraud unravels all (*S.P. Chengalvaraya Naidu*; *A.V. Papayya Sastry*).

b. The 90-day direction in OP(C) 3278/2025 was complied with in **form** but defeated in **substance**: a bare "closed in light of the affidavit" disposal, obtained by misrepresentation, is not the genuine disposal-on-merits the direction contemplated.

c. The alternative remedy is illusory: the Petitioner did approach the trial court (rejoinder; IA 5/2026; IA 6/2026), yet no effective adjudication has followed ⚠ *(insert docket dates / postings)*, and the trial court's order imposed no consequence despite the contradiction.

d. The falsity is **apparent on the face of the record** — established by the Respondents' own documents — and requires no trial, warranting supervisory intervention.

## V. Prayer

It is therefore most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

**a. (Primary)** Set aside the order dated 04.03.2026 closing IA No. 5/2025 in O.S. No. 214/2025 as having been procured by a false affidavit, and direct genuine reconsideration of the production/discovery applications with discovery on oath;

**b. (Alternative, without setting aside)** Direct the trial court to dispose of IA No. 6/2026 and the pending applications **expeditiously, within a fixed time, by a reasoned order on merits**;

**c. (In any event)** Record the contradiction between the affidavit dated 16.02.2026 and the production dated 18.05.2026, and direct the trial court to take the said contradiction and the 18.05.2026 production into account in deciding the applications;

**d.** Grant liberty to the Petitioner to pursue appropriate proceedings (including under Section 340 CrPC / Section 379 BNSS and for recall) in respect of the false affidavit, and leave it open to this Hon'ble Court to take such action as it deems fit in respect thereof;

**e.** Direct maintenance of **status quo** in respect of the Trust property and the commercial venture pending disposal of the applications;

**f.** Costs, and such other relief as this Hon'ble Court deems fit.

### Drafting notes (not part of the filing)

- **Anchor on "not available," not "dormant."** Point 1 (sworn unavailable → produced) is the unanswerable core and does not depend on winning the dormancy-window argument. Lead with it.
- **⚠ markers = verify before filing.** Every quoted phrase and paragraph pin (16.02.2026 affidavit; O.S. 243/2024; the Deed; lease/registration office; signboard photo; GSTIN) must be checked character-for-character and pin-for-pin against the originals. The petition's force is "Respondents misstate the record" — a single wrong quote on the Petitioner's side undercuts it.
- **Do not plead "disobedience" of the HC order.** It was complied with in form (disposed within 90 days). Plead defeat-of-substance / abuse of process instead.
- **Maradu/Cheppad and Sree Moolavasam control / FEMA** are pleaded as *inference* and *matters warranting inquiry*, not as found facts — so the Court can note them without having to find them.
- **Section IV(c) needs the actual docket dates** of IA 6/2026 and the rejoinder postings to convert "no speedy action" from an adjective into a provable record.
- **Confirm representation/vakalat** is clean for the HC (Adv. on record), independent of the trial-court appearances (Adv. M. Thaha / Adv. Perot).
- Reliefs are laid in a **descending ladder** so the Court may grant the alternative/record-and-remit relief even if it declines the set-aside — capturing the near-certain outcome while preserving the strong one.
