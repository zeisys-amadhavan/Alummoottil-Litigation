## Statement of Facts

**1.** That, the plaintiff herein is a private family trust constituted as per the registered deed No. 17/IV/2024. Including the managing trustee, 5 trustees are there in the plaintiff trust.

**2.** That, the properties scheduled hereunder belong to the plaintiff trust by virtue of 3 deeds, namely Settlement Deed No. 1669/I/2022 dated 10/11/2022, Settlement Deed Number 298/I/2024 dated 07/03/2024, and Settlement Deed No. 299/I/2024 dated 07/03/2024. Plaint scheduled property is now under the absolute possession and enjoyment of the plaintiff trust.

**3.** That, at present, the total extent of plaint scheduled property is 38.06 ares, which includes 21.81 ares in Resurvey No. 219/11, 0.65 ares in Resurvey No. 219/29, 4.75 ares in Resurvey No. 219/19, 2.01 ares in Resurvey No. 219/28-1, 0.11 ares in Resurvey No. 219/27-1, 5.39 ares in Resurvey No. 219/11-1, and 3.25 ares in Resurvey No. 219/10. Said properties are lying together to form the compact plot of plaint scheduled property. It is hereby submitted that three buildings are there in the plaint scheduled property. Among the said 3 buildings the main building is 'Meda' and is at the eastern portion of the entire plaint scheduled property. The remaining 2 buildings are at the western side of the main building and are known as 'Nalukettu' and 'Nelpura'. Now the administration and management of plaint scheduled property and the buildings therein are vested with the plaintiff Trust. Now certain renovation works for the above said buildings are going on at the auspices of the plaintiff trust.

**4.** That, the defendant herein is the son of Mr. Radhakrishnan M, who is none other than the 2nd trustee of the plaintiff trust. Apart from that relationship the defendant herein doesn't have any connection with the plaintiff trust. He is not a member of the plaintiff trust also. As such the defendant doesn't have any right or authority to interfere with the affairs of the plaintiff trust and to intimidate the right of the plaintiff trust over the plaint scheduled property and the above said buildings in it. But from the very inception of the plaintiff trust the defendant takes a hostile attitude towards the plaintiff trust, and he is frequently trying to trespass into the plaint scheduled property and to intimidate the right of the plaintiff trust over the plaint scheduled property and the buildings therein. As a part of the same, the defendant herein along with 5 other men trespassed into the plaint scheduled property on 09.08.2024, tried to obstruct the construction works which are being carried out in the plaint scheduled property at the instance of the plaintiff trust, and brutally manhandled 3 employees deployed by the plaintiff trust. In connection with said incident, Kareelakulangara Police has registered a crime against the defendant as Crime No. 574/2024; a copy of the FIR and of the FIS in the said crime is submitted herewith. Kareelakulangara Police has registered said crime against the defendant by alleging the commission of the offences punishable under 115(2), 118(1), 296(b) and 3(5) of Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita. After committing the atrocities as mentioned above, the defendant left the place after threatening that he will not allow the plaintiff trust to proceed with the ongoing construction activities in the plaint scheduled property.

**5.** That, thereafter on 13.08.2024 the plaintiff has got reliable information from believable sources that the defendant is making hasty preparations to trespass into the plaint scheduled property again, and to cause obstructions to the ongoing constructions in the plaint scheduled property. The defendant doesn't have any right or authority to indulge in any such activities. But in the event of any such illegal acts from the side of the defendant, the plaintiff is quite unable to resist the same. So the plaintiff has no option other than to approach this Hon'ble Court with this suit, and the plaintiff hereby humbly prays to restrain the defendant and his men permanently by a decree of permanent prohibitory injunction and temporarily till the disposal of this suit by an order of ad-interim injunction, from trespassing into the plaint scheduled property and the buildings therein, from committing any waste or mischief in the plaint scheduled property, and from causing any obstruction or hurdles to the ongoing construction activities in the plaint scheduled property. Otherwise the plaintiff will be put to irreparable loss, injury and hardship.

**6.** That, the cause of action for this suit has arisen on 13.08.2024, the date on which the plaintiff has got reliable information that the defendant is making hasty preparations to trespass into the plaint scheduled property again, at Cheppad Village wherein the plaint scheduled property and the buildings therein situate, and which is within the territorial jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court.

**7. Suit Valuation and Court Fee:** For injunction this suit is valued up to ₹500/- and for which a court fee of ₹20/- is paid under Section 27(c) of the Kerala Court Fee and Suit Valuation Act. Apart from the said court fee, an amount of Rs. 100/- is also paid as Legal Benefit Fund Fee.

## Prayer

Hence it is most humbly and respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to pass a decree allowing the following reliefs:

1. To restrain the defendant and his men permanently by a decree of permanent prohibitory injunction and temporarily till the disposal of this suit by an order of ad-interim injunction, from trespassing into the plaint scheduled property and the buildings therein, from committing any waste or mischief in the plaint scheduled property, and from causing any obstruction or hurdles to the ongoing construction activities in the plaint scheduled property.
2. To allow the plaintiff to realize the cost of this litigation from the defendant and his assets.
3. To allow such other reliefs as this Honourable Court deems fit and proper for the interest of justice.
