## Paragraph 2 — Characterisation of the counter-affidavit

The counter-affidavit filed by the 2nd Defendant (Managing Trustee) is an evasion of the statutory fiduciary duties imposed on Trustees and a fundamental violation of the prayers in IA No. 5/2025 as well as the directions of the Hon'ble High Court to dispose of this IA within 90 days from 15.01.2026.

## Paragraph 3 — Contradictions from the defendants' own sworn statements

The following facts and contradictions are brought to the notice of this Hon'ble Court from the defendants' own sworn statements:

### 3(a) — Dormancy vs active construction

Defendant 2 states in his counter-affidavit (Paras 4-6) that the Trust was a *"dormant entity"* with *"zero funding"* and *"no accounts."* This is directly contradicted by his own sworn statement in O.S. 243/2024 (Paras 3-5) filed in this very honorable court, that active construction by the Trust and *"employees deployed by the plaintiff trust"* existed as of 09.08.2024. These facts show the operationalising of an unaccounted-for enterprise and undermine the legal basis of O.S. 243/2024 filed by Defendant 2 against the Plaintiff.

### 3(b) — No books of account vs admitted construction

Defendant 2 admits in his counter-affidavit (Paras 4-5) that *"no books of account were maintained"* for 17 years. Because the defendants simultaneously state in O.S. 243/2024 (Para 4) that *"construction works which are being carried out in the plaint scheduled property at the instance of plaintiff trust"*, this contradiction shows a violation of the mandatory duty to keep accounts under Section 19 of the Indian Trusts Act, 1882.

### 3(c) — Contractors / artisans without vouchers

Defendant 2 states that renovation involved contractors, artisans, architects and material suppliers, while confirming *"no vouchers or records"* were produced. This failure to maintain a paper trail for capital improvements has serious implications under GST and Income Tax laws and violates the duty of care under Section 15 of the Indian Trusts Act.

### 3(d) — Renovation by D3's personal accounts; foreign remittance concern

Defendant 2 states renovation was funded by the *"personal accounts"* of Defendant 3 (who is not a citizen of India) without being routed through Trust bank accounts or records. This raises serious concerns under foreign inward remittance laws and constitutes an admitted *"blending"* of property under Section 66 of the Indian Trusts Act.

### 3(e) — D3 "no obligation to provide accounts" vs Section 57 ITA

Defendant 2 claims Defendant 3 had *"no obligation to provide accounts."* This directly conflicts with the beneficiary's absolute statutory right under Section 57 of the Indian Trusts Act.

### 3(f) — Personnel resolutions vs employees deployed

Defendant 2 states (Para 13) that no resolutions exist for personnel, yet his Plaint in O.S. 243/2024 (Para 5) admits *"employees deployed by the plaintiff trust"* were active on 09.08.2024. This shows that the personnel present on the property were not authorised Trust employees, but unauthorised private enforcers, deployed without formal trustee resolution, which led to the events of 09.08.2024 that are the subject matter of O.S. 243/2024 filed by the defendants.

### 3(g) — Five vague resolutions for a 17-year period

Defendant 2 has produced only five vague resolutions for a 17-year period. The omission of specific authorizations for the *"employees"* and *"construction"* mentioned in O.S. 243/2024 demonstrates a violation of the duty of transparency under Section 11 of the Indian Trusts Act.

### 3(h) — "Not a beneficiary" vs 2007 Deed definition

Defendant 2 asserts (Para 11) that the Plaintiff is *"not a beneficiary,"* yet Paragraph 4 of the 2007 Deed explicitly defines beneficiaries as *"descendants of Alummoottil Chellamma Channathy."* Having elsewhere admitted the Plaintiff is the grandson of the said Chellamma, and that the 2007 Trust Deed is the founding instrument of the trust, this contradiction has serious implications regarding the accuracy of statements made on oath by Defendant 2 before this Hon'ble Court. And Defendant 2 repeats these inaccuracies on all ongoing sworn statements.

### 3(i) — Sreemoolavasam disclosure

The defendants state in their counter-affidavit dated 16.02.2026 that the Trust was dormant with zero funding and no accounts. Sreemoolavasam Wellness and Leisure Retreats Private Limited (GSTIN: 32ABQCS9416G1Z1) was registered and started operating at the exact Trust address after its GSTIN registration on 09.12.2025. This chronology, together with the current operation of a private enterprise providing residential health-care services on the Trust premises while the trust is claimed to be dormant, has serious implications for the credibility of the statements made on oath and for the claim of dormancy itself.

### 3(j) — "Absolute possession" and "immediate control" admission

Defendant 2 admits in O.S. 243/2024 that he exercised *"absolute possession"* and *"immediate control"* over the Trust property. As the Managing Trustee under whose leadership the 17-year vacuum of accounts occurred, this has implications for Defendant 2's personal liability under Section 23 of the Indian Trusts Act, and various provisions of the law.
