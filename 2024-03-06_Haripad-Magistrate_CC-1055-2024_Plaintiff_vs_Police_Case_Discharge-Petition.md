## PETITION UNDER SECTION 239 OF THE BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA, 2023 (BNSS) FOR DISCHARGE OF THE ACCUSED

**The petitioner respectfully submits as follows:**

1. The petitioner is the 1st accused in **C.C. No. 1055/2024** on the file of this Hon'ble Court, which arose from Crime No. 0574/2024 of **Kareelakulangara Police Station**, alleging offences under **Sections 115(2), 118(1), 3(5) and 296(b) of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**.

2. The final report has been filed by the police on the basis of a false and motivated complaint lodged by the de-facto complainant, who is working as a **supervisor of the building construction of Alummootil Meda**, a property belonging to **Alummootil Tharavadu Trust**, of which the petitioner is a **lawful beneficiary**.

3. On **09.08.2024 at about 11.00 a.m.**, during the petitioner's short visit to Kerala, he went to **Alummootil Meda**, his childhood home, only to see the ancestral property. The de-facto complainant **restrained him from entering** the premises without any authority or justification. In the altercation that followed, the petitioner himself sustained **grievous bodily injuries**. The petitioner sought medical assistance from Govt hospital, Haripad on 09.08.2024. Then he sought assistance from Huda Trust hospital. In order to **escape his own liability and criminal acts**, the complainant concocted a false version and lodged a complaint against the petitioner.

4. The **Scene Mahazar** produced along with the final report reveals that it was prepared on **10.08.2024 at 11.00 a.m., prior to the registration of the FIR**, which is shown as having been registered only at **5.13 p.m. on 10.08.2024**. This chronological inconsistency clearly indicates **fabrication and manipulation** in the investigation process and renders the entire prosecution case doubtful.

5. The records further reveal that the complainant was reportedly admitted in hospital at **11.50 a.m. on 09.08.2024**, as revealed from the wound certificate filed along with the final report. The complainant's **statement was recorded from Taluk Hospital, Haripad at 3.30 p.m. on 10.08.2024**, while he is also shown to have been **present at Kareelakulangara Police Station at 11.00 a.m. on the same day**. The contradiction regarding his whereabouts proves the falsity of the complaint and vitiates the credibility of the investigation.

6. The petitioner had immediately **called Police Helpline 112** after being restrained on 09.08.2024. Police responded to that call and came to the spot. However, this crucial fact and the corresponding police response have been **suppressed in the FIR and final report**, further evidencing bias and unfair investigation.

7. The entire case is the outcome of **personal vengeance**. The complainant acts at the instance of the petitioner's uncle, who harbours animosity towards the petitioner owing to a **civil dispute** pending between them in **O.S. No. 243/2024** before the Civil Court, wherein the said uncle unsuccessfully sought to restrain the petitioner from entering the Tharavad property. Having failed in that attempt, the present false criminal case was engineered to harass and humiliate the petitioner.

8. Even if the entire prosecution materials are accepted as true, **no ingredients of the alleged offences** under Sections 115(2), 118(1), 3(5) or 296(b) of BNS are made out against the petitioner. The allegations are **inherently improbable, mala fide and civil in nature**, and therefore, continuance of the proceedings would amount to **abuse of the process of law**.

9. The petitioner is innocent and has not committed any offence. The continuation of the proceedings against him would cause **serious miscarriage of justice**.

---

**Hence, it is most humbly prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:**

(a) Discharge the petitioner/accused in **C.C. No. 1055/2024** under **Section 239 of the BNSS**, and

(b) Pass such other and further orders as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the interest of justice.
