O.S. 214/2025
I.A. 5/2026 (status quo)
I.A. 6/2026 (discovery)

What “status quo” means
    1) “Status quo” is being misquoted. The prayer is clear. It asks the Court to “maintain status quo, restrain commercial ventures, events, inaugurations, and non-trust public entry.” The Defendants quote only the first two words and ignore the rest. This misstates the relief actually sought.
    2) The baseline is the property as it stood on the date of filing (12/06/2025). O.S. 214/2025 was filed to keep the property safe while the suit is decided. Instead, the Defendants enabled a private company to operate from it without any permission from the Court. A change made during the suit cannot become the baseline the Court protects.
        A) The Plaintiff is excluded from entry. He cannot know the internal use or any alteration. That exclusion is precisely why status quo must mean preservation. It cannot mean a private company operating behind closed gates.

THEIR “status quo” is concealment and defiance
    3) This is self-help during pending litigation, not status quo. Three suits over this property are pending before this Court: O.S. 214/2025, O.S. 243/2024 and O.S. 84/2024. All three predate the private company. While they were pending, the Defendants leased and commercialised the property. To call that “status quo” is to ask the Court to sanction the disregard of its own authority.
    4) The concealment was deliberate. The 2nd Defendant executed the lease on 27/10/2025 during the pending suits and while I.A. 5/2025 sought the Trust’s accounts. The Plaintiff uncovered it and disclosed it in his rejoinder. But for that disclosure, the lease and its income would have remained hidden.
        A) The private company was not invisible. It was GST-registered on 09/12/2025 with its principal place of business at the Trust address. As of 01/03/2026, its name board stands in front of the Tharavad. It was public-facing, yet concealed from the Plaintiff and this Court.
    5) A status quo cannot arise from suppression. This Court closed I.A. 5/2025 “in light of the affidavit” dated 16/02/2026 in which the Defendant swore the documents were unavailable. That covered the bank statements and financial records now produced; the lease too was already in existence. What was concealed from the Court cannot later become a protected status quo.
        A) They closed I.A. 5/2025 by denying the records. They now ask the Court to protect that same lease as “status quo.” The denial that won the first order cannot now be rewarded by protecting what it hid.
        B) Compelled disclosure creates no rights. Production was selective and came only after a sworn plea of non-availability. That warrants an adverse inference, not the Court’s indulgence.

The Court has already been misled on this lease
    6) The I.A. 5/2025 closure, made under the High Court’s time-bound direction, now rests on a disproved affidavit. The lease, bank statements and minutes book surfaced on 18/05/2026 — after the High Court’s window had passed.
        A) The contradiction is documentary. All these documents existed before the 16/02/2026 affidavit. The Managing Trustee who swore they were unavailable later produced them himself.
    7) This is a repeat performance. I.A. 1/2024 in O.S. 243/2024 was argued on 27/11/2025. The Trust told the Court it alone possessed the property. The lease had already been signed a month earlier. They never mentioned it.
        A) On 03/12/2025 this Court dismissed the Trust’s I.A. 1/2024. It found no prima facie case for the Trust, that the balance of convenience favoured the Plaintiff, and that the Trust would suffer no real harm if the injunction was refused.

A PATTERN OF SELF-HELP MUST NOT BE REWARDED
    8) The Defendants have used self-help before, during pending litigation. The Plaintiff filed O.S. 84/2024 on 06/03/2024 seeking protection against obstruction of access. That suit was sub judice. On 09/08/2024 the Defendants deployed outside enforcers with no tie to the Trust or family. They obstructed the Plaintiff’s entry and assaulted him.
        A) They then set the criminal law in motion against him through FIR 574/2024. That case is now pending as CC 1055/2024.
        B) They used that false case to found O.S. 243/2024, filed on 14/08/2024. It falsely claimed the Plaintiff has “no connection with the Trust” and suppressed Deed 2/IV/2007.
    9) They are repeating the same strategy. They sought exclusive possession through I.A. 1/2024. It was refused on 03/12/2025. Days later, on 09/12/2025, the company was GST-registered at the Trust address. They cannot manufacture privately the exclusivity the Court had just declined to grant.
        A) To protect this would defeat the 03/12/2025 order. It would pre-empt all three pending suits. The Court should not reward a proven pattern.

No fiduciary authority
    10) No fiduciary power without accountability. The 2nd Defendant’s authority as Managing Trustee comes only from the Trust Deed and the Indian Trusts Act, 1882. He has withheld the Trust accounts for eighteen years. A trustee who has not accounted for eighteen years cannot, in the nineteenth, ask the Court to protect his concealed commercialisation.
    11) No authority is shown for the lease. The Defendants’ own First Amendment (66/IV/2022, Clause 17) permits leasing only with the consent of all trustees and beneficiaries. The Plaintiff’s consent was never taken. This is the same Managing Trustee who withheld the foundational Deed 2/IV/2007. The High Court had to compel its production on 27/08/2025.

The lease is void
    12) The lease is void for want of registration jurisdiction. The Trust property lies in Cheppad, Alappuzha. Yet it was registered at Maradu SRO, Ernakulam. Maradu had no jurisdiction under Section 28 of the Registration Act, 1908. The registration is void and the lease is inadmissible under Section 49.
    13) The registering office was falsely described. In the affidavit dated 18/05/2026 the 2nd Defendant called it the “Marad, Alappuzha Sub-Registrar’s Office.” No such office exists.
    14) Every Trust deed was registered at Cheppad SRO. This lease alone was taken to Maradu. This was not good faith. It kept the transaction away from local scrutiny, the Plaintiff, and this Court.

Discovery (I.A. 6/2026)
    15) I.A. 6/2026 is not a second bite. It is the direct answer to the false “dormant trust / no documents” affidavit. This Trust amended deeds, renovated buildings, paid fees, claimed employees, opened a bank account, filed O.S. 243/2024 and leased the property. It cannot say no records exist.
        A) Their own production is internally unreliable. The 16/02/2026 reply says the account started on 26/05/2025. The 18/05/2026 application says 28/05/2025. The statement period is shown as 28/05/2005 to 15/05/2026 — twenty years off.
        B) The Defendants’ facts change with convenience. In O.S. 243/2024 the Trust was pleaded under Reg. No. 17/IV/2024. In the 18/05/2026 application it becomes Reg. No. 2/IV/2007.

Consequence
    16) An adverse inference is warranted. Documents were first withheld on oath as unavailable. They were later produced selectively after the closure of I.A. 5/2025.

Injunction tests
    17) Prima facie case. The Plaintiff’s beneficiary right is prima facie established by Deed 2/IV/2007. On 03/12/2025 this Court itself could not accept that he has no right over the property. The purported lease was suppressed, executed pendente lite, rests on no clean authority, and contradicts the Defendants’ own affidavit of dormancy.
        A) The Court need not finally decide the lease’s validity. It need only hold that a suppressed, pendente lite lease cannot be treated as protected status quo.
    18) Balance of convenience. The Defendants’ commercialisation was concealed. Any inconvenience is of their own making. A lessee who entered pendente lite assumed the risk. Lis pendens applies.
    19) Irreparable injury. The Plaintiff is excluded from his ancestral property while a private company operates publicly from it. Loss of access and accountability cannot be repaired by damages.

The status quo to restore
    20) The last lawful status quo is the position before the concealed lease of 27/10/2025 — no private commercial venture, no non-trust public entry, no commercial exploitation. Restraining the trustees asks nothing extraordinary. It holds fiduciaries to their existing duty to preserve the Trust property pending suit.
        A) The freeze should continue until the Defendants fully account for the Trust, prove their authority for the lease, disclose the full transaction, and deposit into Court all rents, advances and other sums received to date.
        B) The Plaintiff seeks no direct relief against the lessee. The restraint runs against the trustees alone.
