# O.S. 214/2025 — Case at a Glance

## Why

| Grievance | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Beneficiary — status denied** | The Plaintiff is a lineal descendant of Chellamma Channathy and a rightful beneficiary, yet the defendants deny it. |
| **Trust deed — suppressed** | D2 conceals the original Deed 2/IV/2007 to defeat the Plaintiff's rights; copies refused despite repeated requests. |
| **Amendments — forged** | Deeds 66/IV/2022 and 17/IV/2024 were registered without beneficiary consent to redefine the beneficiaries and exclude him — pleaded void. |
| **Access — unlawful exclusion** | D2–D4 barred the Plaintiff from his ancestral home (Meda, Nalukettu, Nelpura) through private enforcers, with no court order. |
| **Assault — premeditated attack** | On 09.08.2024 hired enforcers (D5–D7 and others), directed by D2–D4, attacked the Plaintiff — a torn rotator cuff needing surgery. |
| **Fiduciary — duties delegated** | Trust control was handed to outsiders with no trust role (s.47); D3, USA-domiciled, is disqualified as a trustee (s.60). |
| **False FIR — FIR 574/2024** | A retaliatory, fabricated FIR to reverse blame, shield D6–D7 and brand the Plaintiff a trespasser. |
| **Sham suit — O.S. 243/2024** | Built on a fabricated cause of action (he had left India before the alleged date) and the suppressed deed; trespass alleged despite no entry. |
| **Heirlooms — protected items missing** | A mounted bison skull and deer antlers vanished from the Meda under the defendants' custody — possibly taken abroad. |
| **Accounts — 18 years, none** | D2 has never disclosed audited trust accounts (ss.19, 57, 59); trust funds were used to fund litigation against a beneficiary. |
| **Self-dealing — trust as fiefdom** | D2 and D4 acquired land abutting the Meda for personal control — a conflict of interest (ss.51, 66). |
| **Relief — removal sought** | The Plaintiff seeks D2's removal as Managing Trustee and enforceable beneficiary rights to the Meda, Nalukettu and Nelpura. |
