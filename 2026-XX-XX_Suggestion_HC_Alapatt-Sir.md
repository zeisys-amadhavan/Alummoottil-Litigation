# Note for Adv. Unnikrishnan V. Alapatt — High Court

*Dear Sir, this sets out the facts as I understand them and what I am hoping to achieve, for your consideration. The approach is entirely a matter for your judgment.*

1. **What I am hoping for.** The High Court has already directed the lower court to decide three of my applications within three months, and that has not happened. My hope is that the High Court might move the lower court to hear and decide them, and that the other side be required to produce the relevant documents.

2. **The fact I think matters most.** On 16 February 2026 the other side filed a sworn statement that the documents I had sought were "not available for production before the court." On 18 May 2026 they produced those very documents. Their own production appears to show the earlier sworn statement was untrue.

3. **A second fact in the same vein.** In an earlier suit of theirs (August 2024) they swore the trust is "constituted as per the registered deed No. 17/IV/2024," while in their February 2026 statement and again in May 2026 they swear it was registered on 04-01-2007 as 2/IV/2007. As I understand it a trust carries a single registration number, so one of these appears to be incorrect.

4. These two points seem to me the strongest, in that they rest on the other side's own papers rather than on anything that would need witnesses or investigation — though you will know whether that is how best to present them.

5. **A concern I'd ask you to weigh.** I am anxious that nothing in our case read as criticism of the judge. My understanding is that the lower court acted in good faith on a sworn statement it was entitled to accept; the difficulty seems to me to lie in the other side's conduct, not the court's.

6. **A point on the record I'd value your reading of.** On 4 March 2026 the court recorded "Petition closed in light of the affidavit. For hearing IAs." My own reading is that this closed the pleadings but did not decide my applications, which remain pending — but I may be wrong, and I would not want us to describe the applications as having been "dismissed" if the record does not bear that out.

7. On that reading, what I am really seeking is simply that the applications be heard and decided, with the documents produced. (The exact status of application No. 5 is still being checked at the file; we may need an alternative position on it.)

8. **On why the three months lapsed.** As I understand the file, the court itself moved promptly — taking the matter up on its own on 6 February — and the time then ran out during a Supreme Court conference the judge attended (9 March), a declared holiday (9 April), and the court vacation (to 18 May). I mention this because the record also shows I was absent on a couple of dates and that my applications were unserved for a period, and I would not want us to overstate my own diligence and have that turned against us — but how to handle it is for you.

9. **Further facts that may support the central point**, for whatever use they are: they produced current-year documents that had been swept into "not available"; they produced the original minutes book in May, which would mean it existed in February; the lease they produced is dated 27 October 2025, some months before they swore the trust was "dormant"; and they swore the trust had "no revenue from any source whatsoever," while in their own earlier suit they described it running construction with "3 employees deployed by the plaintiff trust" and holding property transferred to it under three settlement deeds.

10. **A wider pattern**, which I raise only as context: the same posture runs across three related suits — all before the same court, the same parties, the same trust and the same property. I am conscious it may be wiser to keep this as background showing the conduct is not isolated, rather than something for the High Court to rule on, but I leave that to you.

11. **What I would value, in rough order:** that the lower court be moved to hear and decide my applications, with the documents produced, in a fixed time; failing that, prompt disposal; that the contradiction between the February statement and the May production be recorded; that the other side's conduct in the related suits be available on the question of their credibility; and that the three related suits be heard together.

12. **The registration of the lease.** Our property is in Cheppad, yet the lease over it was registered at Marad, which I understand to be in Ernakulam — neither where the property lies nor the Cheppad office. I would be glad if the Marad registrar could be asked to explain this. I can set out the timeline and the fact that the High Court had earlier directed the Cheppad office about our deeds; I'd leave any inference as to why to speak for itself.

13. I would also be grateful if the property and the venture could be kept as they are until the applications are decided, if that is open to us.

14. **One thing I'm very conscious of:** because our whole concern is that the other side has misstated the record, the accuracy of our own quotations and dates feels especially important, and I have tried to give you their exact words.

15. In that connection, I should flag that an earlier filing of mine (13 March) put quotation marks around phrases that are not in fact in their sworn statement — "zero funding," "no accounts," and the like. I would rather we rely only on their exact words, which seem to me damaging enough.

16. A few small things I'll make consistent on my side: "three months" rather than "90 days," consistent ages and dates, and the beneficiary wording quoted exactly from the 2007 deed.

17. The one fact still being confirmed before anything is filed is whether application No. 5 was decided or only "pleadings closed" — Thaha is obtaining the order sheet and register so we can be definite rather than hedge.

18. **On sequence**, a thought for your view: it may be worth approaching the lower court first with a short request that, since the documents earlier said to be unavailable have now been produced, my document application be taken up — and turning to the High Court only if that does not move things. I'd be guided by you and Thaha on timing.
