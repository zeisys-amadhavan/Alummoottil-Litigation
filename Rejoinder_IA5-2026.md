## B. Prayer (a) is not infructuous.

D2 calls prayer (a) *"infructuous"* because I.As 2/2025, 5/2025 and 9/2025 were *"disposed off"* on 04.03.2026. But that order merely *"closed [the petitions] in light of the affidavit"* of 16.02.2026 — an affidavit falsified by D2's own documents:

i. *"nothing material has been concealed"* [para 14] — yet he held Lease 3237/I/2025 (registered 27.10.2025), produced only on 18.05.2026.
ii. *"complete account records… not available"* [para 13] — yet he produced the statement of A/c 13100200004482 (admitted, para 8) himself on 18.05.2026.
iii. *"unable to produce these documents"* [para 13] — yet he produced the original Minutes Book itself on 18.05.2026.
iv. *"no assets/liabilities"* 2007–2024 [para 6] — yet Minutes Book p.19 records Ravindran's ₹1,00,00,000, contributed before his death on 27.08.2022 (p.5).
v. *"not received any funding… from any source whatsoever"* 2007–2024 [para 4] — yet Minutes Book p.11 records ₹1 crore to the construction account, 12.01.2024.

A closure resting on a false affidavit decides nothing and bars nothing (*Sharada Sanghi v. Asha Agarwal*, 2026 INSC 292; *S.P. Chengalvaraya Naidu v. Jagannath*, (1994) 1 SCC 1); the High Court's direction to *dispose of* the I.As (OP(C) 3278/2025, 15.01.2026) remains unfulfilled. Prayer (a), conditioned on their genuine disposal, is therefore live.

The Plaintiff seeks no revision, trusting this Hon'ble Court to revisit its own closure and dispose of the I.As on merits — not that the closure was wrong, but that its premise has collapsed. Prayers (b) and (c) D2 does not address at all.

## D. The lessee is not a necessary party.

Per Objection, 19.06.2026, **para 3**, the lessee *"is also a necessary party."* It is not.

**(i)** Every relief is sought against the respondent trustees alone — all on record. None is sought against the lessee, so it need not be joined. The restraint binds the lessor *in personam*; a lease the lessor is enjoined from performing cannot be performed by the lessee, so the interim/final distinction is beside the point.

**(ii)** The lease (27.10.2025) is a transfer *pendente lite*: the Meda was already the subject of three pending suits when D2 leased it —

**(a)** **O.S. 84/2024 (06.03.2024)** — the Plaintiff's suit seeking a declaration of his right over the Meda and an injunction against documents dealing with it; the property itself is directly in issue.

**(b)** **O.S. 243/2024 (14.08.2024)** — D2's *own* suit, asserting the Trust's *"absolute possession and enjoyment"* of the Meda; having invoked that court over this property, D2 was bound to tell it he was leasing the property — and did not.

**(c)** **O.S. 214/2025 (12.06.2025)** — the present suit; here D2 not merely concealed the lease but swore, on 16.02.2026, that *"nothing material has been concealed."*

A *pendente lite* transferee is neither necessary nor proper and need not be joined; it is bound by the result regardless (Section 52 TPA; O.1 R.9 CPC; *Sarvinder Singh v. Dalip Singh*, (1996) 5 SCC 539; *Thomson Press v. Nanak Builders*, (2013) 5 SCC 397).

**(iii)** In any event the lessee did not exist until 07.08.2025 — it could not have been joined to suits filed in 2024 and June 2025. The Plaintiff cannot be faulted for failing to sue a company that was yet to be born.

The lease's validity is not admitted.

## E. Non-joinder of the trustees is no bar.

**(i)** Per Objection, 19.06.2026, **para 4**, all five trustees *"must be made party."* They need not be: the reliefs run against the Trust (D1), on record through D2; non-joinder defeats no petition (O.1 R.9 CPC).

**(ii)** Nor can D2 raise it. He sued for the Trust alone (O.S. 243/2024), defends for it alone (O.S. 214/2025), and executed the lease alone (27.10.2025). Having *been* the Trust whenever it suited him, he cannot now hide behind the other four.

**(iii)** D2 signed the lease on 27.10.2025, then had his own act placed for *"ratification"* 89 days later (Minutes, p.17). One seeks ratification only for what was done without authority; here it is admission enough that D2 made the lease alone.

**(iv)** The terms were D2's alone — lessee, flat ₹60,000 rent, ten-year term, no escalation, no deposit (Clauses 2–3; no such clauses exist), ratified only later as *"the action taken by Managing Trustee"* (Minutes, 24.01.2026, p.17). He even let the lessee register the Meda itself as its GST premises (2/242, w.e.f. 09.12.2025) — entrenching a stranger on trust property as his solo act.

## F. Misjoinder of D5–D7 is no bar.

Per Objection, 19.06.2026, **para 5**, the petition is *"barred by misjoinder"* of D5–D7. It is not: *"no decree shall be reversed or substantially varied… on account of any misjoinder… of parties"* (Section 99 CPC; O.1 R.9 CPC). At most a wrongly joined name is struck; nothing is sought against D5–D7, and the order stands without them.

## G. The triple test favours restraint.

Per Objection, 19.06.2026, **para 12**, D2 pleads the Plaintiff has *"no prima facia case,"* balance favours the defendants, and status quo works them *"irreparable lose."* Each limb this Court has already answered — against D2.

**(i) Prima facie case.** On 03.12.2025, dismissing D2's injunction over this same property, this Court held the Plaintiff would be *"denuded from enjoying his right over plaint schedule property"* and that *"plaintiffs have failed to establish a prima facie case"* (I.A. 1/2024 in O.S. 243/2024, paras 9–10). D2's Minutes (24.01.2026, p.20) then resolve to *"terminate"* that right. The *"no prima facia case"* D2 pleads is the finding this Court returned against him.

**(ii) Irreparable injury.** The harm cannot be undone by money. First, accountability itself: once the public enters and the venture runs, the trust's financial and inventory state before commercialisation is lost forever — the very loss I.A. 5/2026 exists to prevent, *"which cannot later be cured by damages."* And the property's character: the Meda (built 1906) and the Nalukettu (built 1624) — as D2 himself declared in Settlement Deed 299/I/2024 (Recital 10 / Form 1B §3), on a Chartered Engineer's valuation, and recorded *"Residential"* in both that deed and the LSGD building register (Building IDs 50494010007872 and …7874) — is defaced for commerce: a *"treatment block"* built and its ground floor partitioned (Minutes, p.17), and thrown open to the public. Antiques and furniture pass to the lessee before any inventory (p.19–20). The Plaintiff, 50, is shut from his childhood home for ten to twenty years (Clauses 2, 13(a)); and the trust's founding object — *"preserving heritage of the family"* (2/IV/2007) — is inverted, the tharavad traded as *"Sreemoolavasam,"* never named Alummoottil (lease; GST; website, to be proved). The registered lease sweeps 29.42 Ares though the Meda's plinth is 4.645 Ares (recital; Clause 17) — the whole compound, not one building. No man of ordinary prudence (s.15, Indian Trusts Act) would do this to property of his own, let alone to property held in trust for another. Pendente lite the nature of the property must be preserved absent irreparable loss to the other side: *"the court should not permit the nature of the property being changed which also includes alienation or transfer"* (*Maharwal Khewaji Trust v. Baldev Dass*, (2004) 8 SCC 488). D2 does both.

**(iii) Balance of convenience.** This Court has already held it — against D2. On 03.12.2025 it found *"balance of convenience is not in favour of"* the petitioners (I.A. 1/2024 in O.S. 243/2024, para 10):

**Plaintiff's side:** A beneficiary by descent, 50, raised in the Meda — barred from his home on 09.08.2024 and prosecuted (Crime 574/2024) by men D2 swore, in his own plaint (O.S. 243/2024, para 4), were *"3 employees deployed by the plaintiff trust"* — the very men he now pleads *"are no way connected to Alummoottil Trust"* (Objection, para 5). The trust exists to protect the Plaintiff — Chellamma Channathy's grandson — the Meda (built 1906) and the Nalukettu (built 1624) (Deed 299/I/2024, Recital 10 / Form 1B §3), four centuries of family heritage.

**D2's side:** Nineteen years, no accounts. Nearly ₹12 crore spent, not one GST invoice. A lease D2 signed alone, then swore to this Court nothing was concealed. His whole pleaded "loss" is ₹60,000 a month — ₹7.2 lakh a year, no deposit, no escalation: 1.66% against the property's fair value, 0.45% against the ₹12 crore D2 says he spent — either way, less than a bank deposit yields. And not a rupee reaches the trust: ₹1.47 crore of debt he ran up on those same "zero accounts" swallows the rent, which even a decade cannot clear. The lessee was 81 days old at signing; operations, 20 weeks.

**This Court's balance stands: against D2.**

## H. Why D2 defends the tenant: a trustee profiting from his own lease.

This head seeks no relief. It answers only the question D2's objection raises — why restraint should trouble him. The Indian Trusts Act answers it: a trustee *"bound in a fiduciary character to protect the interests of another"* who *"enters into any dealings under circumstances in which his own interests are, or may be, adverse"* to the trust's, and thereby gains any pecuniary advantage, *"must hold for the benefit of"* the beneficiaries what he gains (Section 88; Illustration (b): a trustee who uses trust property for his own business holds the profit for the beneficiary). D2's own documents show that interest:

**1.** Lease Deed 3237/2025 (p. 4, Cl. 2): *"The lease is for a period of 10 years with effect from 27/10/2025."* Minutes Book (24.1.2026, p. 17): *"start the commercial terms with effect from 1/3/2026."* Federal Bank A/c 13100200004482 — only the cover page produced; no entries on record. Which one is being executed — the registered deed or the resolution that contradicts it?

**2.** Trust NOC (24/11/2025): the Trust *"is the lawful owner of the above Building"* (Ext. H-1). KSEB bill in the same filing (15-10-2025): name *"RAMESH CHANDRAN, S/O T K MADHAVAN"* (Ext. H-2). Why is the trustee's personal bill in the tenant's file?

**3.** That same KSEB bill (15-10-2025): units consumed — 0 (Ext. H-2). What business runs on zero electricity?

**4.** Company board resolution: *"no 6 dated 24/11/2025"* (Ext. H-3). D2's NOC: *"Date: 24/11/2025"* (Ext. H-1). Shareholders' decision: *"8TH DAY OF JANUARY 2026"* (Ext. H-4). How were the permission and the action plan signed 45 days before the decision?

**5.** GST record: *"Effective Date of Registration: 09/12/2025 — Principal Place of Business: 2/242, Alumoottil"* (Ext. H-5). Company's own INC-22: office at 2/242 only *"w.e.f 08/01/2026"* (Ext. H-6). How was the company's tax address inside the Trust's building a month before the company was?

**6.** Minutes Book (pp. 16–17): *"MOA was signed"* + four 11-month agreements (₹1,40,000/month) + *"5% … (AGOP) … for 10 year."* Produced before the Court (their list, 16/02/2026): one lease deed. Where are the other five agreements, and where does the money go?

**7.** And the ownership it recites does not match the paper it files: the NOC of 24.11.2025 declares the Trust *"the lawful owner"* (Ext. H-1), while the very bill filed to evidence those premises stands in D2 personally (Ext. H-2). Trust ownership asserted; the trustee's personal document produced to prove it.

These are the dealings of a trustee whose interest is the venture's, not the trust's — the case Section 88 is written for. Whatever D2 draws from this lease he holds for the beneficiaries; his zeal for the lessee's *"loss"* is zeal for his own.

None of this is fresh evidence, and none of it is the Plaintiff's. The company records — the NOC (Ext. H-1), the electricity bill (Ext. H-2), the board resolution (Ext. H-3), the shareholders' resolution (Ext. H-4), and the INC-22 (Ext. H-6) — are the lessee's own filings with the Registrar of Companies, and the registration extract (Ext. H-5) its own GST filing, obtained by any member of the public from the MCA and GST portals for a fee of ₹100 (receipt, Ext. H-7). The lease, the Minutes and the production list are already on the record of this Court, produced by D2 himself. D2 cannot meet his own public filings and his own productions with the complaint that he has not seen them.
