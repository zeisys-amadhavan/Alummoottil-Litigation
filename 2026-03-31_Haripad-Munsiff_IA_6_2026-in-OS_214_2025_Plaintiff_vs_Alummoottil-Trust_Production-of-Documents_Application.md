## Application under Order XI Rules 12 & 14 CPC

The Plaintiff respectfully submits as follows:

1. The Plaintiff had, even prior to the institution of the suit, issued repeated demands through registered communications during October to December 2024 calling upon the Defendants to produce the Trust accounts and financial records. The Defendants failed to respond or comply.

2. Thereafter, the Plaintiff filed IA No. 5/2025 specifically identifying the categories of documents required, including accounts, bank records, and financial statements of the Trust.

3. In their reply affidavit, the Defendants have admitted that no books of account were maintained and no financial statements were prepared or audited, asserted that they are under no obligation to disclose expenditure, and stated that the documents sought in IA No. 5/2025 are "not available for production before the Court".

4. The said stand is directly destructive of the relief already sought in IA No. 5/2025 and makes a specific order under Order XI Rules 12 and 14 CPC necessary.

5. This constitutes not merely non-production, but an express refusal to maintain and disclose accounts, contrary to the duties cast upon trustees.

6. The documents sought in IA No. 5/2025 are in the exclusive possession, custody, and control of the Defendants and are directly relevant to the adjudication of the suit, including the Plaintiff's rights as a beneficiary and the financial and administrative management of the 1st Defendant Trust.

7. The Hon'ble High Court in OP(C) No. 3278/2025, by order dated 15-01-2026, has directed disposal of IA Nos. 2, 5, and 9 within 90 days. In the absence of discovery and production of documents, effective compliance with the said direction is not possible.

8. Under Order XI Rule 12 CPC, this Hon'ble Court is empowered to direct discovery on oath of documents in the possession of a party. Under Order XI Rule 14 CPC, the Court may order production of such documents. In view of the Defendants' express refusal and failure, invocation of these provisions has become necessary.

---

## Prayer

In the above circumstances, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:

a. Direct the Defendants to make discovery on oath of all documents in their possession, custody, or power (including bank records and third-party records under their control) relating to the accounts, income, expenditure, assets, liabilities, bank transactions, and financial dealings of the 1st Defendant Trust.

b. Direct the Defendants to produce all such documents, including books of account, bank statements, ledgers, vouchers, bills, receipts, audits if any, and all supporting financial records.

c. Fix a strict time limit for compliance.

d. Pass such further orders as this Hon'ble Court deems fit in the interests of justice.
