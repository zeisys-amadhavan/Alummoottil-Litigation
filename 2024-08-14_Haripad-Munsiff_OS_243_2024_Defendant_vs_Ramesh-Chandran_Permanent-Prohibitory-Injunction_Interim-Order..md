This petition having been finally heard on 27 November 2025, and the court on the 3/12/2025 passed the following:

## ORDER

Petition for temporary injunction.

**2.** Petition averments in brief are as follows:— Plaintiff is a private family trust constituted as per the registered deed No. 17/2024. Plaint schedule properties belong to plaintiff trust as per Settlement deed Nos. 1669/22, 298/24 and 299/24. Plaint schedule properties with *Meda, Nalukett and Nelpura* are in the absolute possession and enjoyment of the plaintiff trust. The administration and management of the plaint schedule property and the buildings are vested with the plaintiff trust and renovation works are in progress. Defendant is the son of the second trustee of the plaintiff trust. Apart from that relationship, defendant has no right to interfere with the affairs of plaintiff trust and plaint schedule property and the buildings therein. On 09.08.2024, defendant and his men attempted to obstruct the construction works in the plaint schedule property and manhandled the employees therein. Crime No. 574/2024 of Kareelakulangara Police is registered against the defendant. Defendant threatened to further obstruct the ongoing construction. It is reliably learned that defendant is making hasty preparations to trespass into plaint schedule property and to obstruct the ongoing constructions. Hence the above petition for temporary injunction restraining defendant and his men from trespassing into plaint schedule property, obstructing the constructions and committing any waste therein.

**3.** Respondent filed objection contending as follows:— Plaintiff filed the suit by suppressing the existence of Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust Deed No. 2/2007 of Cheppad SRO, the prime and indispensable document in this case. Plaintiff's representative Mr. Ramesh Chandran, who claims to be the Managing Trustee of Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust, is trying to prevent defendant, a beneficiary of the Trust, from entering into the Trust Property. Registered Deed No. 17/2024 has no separate entity apart from Trust No. 2/2007 of SRO, Cheppad. Plaintiffs failed to disclose the audited financial records to defendant despite repeated requests. Deed No. 17/2024 is a sham document made and registered without consent of all the beneficiaries of the Trust and is void ab-initio. Plaintiffs are attempting to take away the rights of the beneficiaries of the Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust by executing Deed No. 17/2024. OS 84/2024 is filed by defendant. It is denied that plaint schedule property is in the absolute possession and enjoyment of plaintiff trust and the buildings therein are vested with them and renovation works are undergoing under the auspices of plaintiff trust. The Alummoottil Meda was fully constructed in November 2023 and inaugurated in December 2023 and has since been open to the public. Defendant, who grew up in the Trust property, has an inherent equitable interest in it and cannot be barred from entering it. Defendant has been out of India since August 11, 2024. The above petition is without any bonafides and is liable to be dismissed.

**4.** The following points arise for consideration:

1. Whether petitioners have made out a prima facie case in support of their claim?
2. In whose favour the balance of convenience exists?
3. Whether the petitioners will be put to irreparable loss and injury if an order of injunction is not passed?
4. Whether the petitioners are entitled to get an interim injunction as prayed for?
5. Reliefs and cost?

**5.** On the side of the petitioners, Exts. A1 to A5 and Ext. C1 series were marked. Exts. B1 to B3 were marked from the side of respondent.

**6.** Heard both sides.

**7.** *Point Nos. 1 to 5:*— Suit is for permanent prohibitory injunction restraining defendant and his men from trespassing into plaint schedule property, obstructing the constructions and committing any waste therein. The contention of plaintiffs is that plaint schedule property with buildings therein are vested with plaintiff trust and defendant, having no right over the same, is attempting to trespass and obstruct the constructions therein. Defendant on the other hand prominently contends that he is a beneficiary of Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust as per deed No. 2/2007 of Cheppad SRO which is suppressed by plaintiffs.

**8.** Ext. A1 is the copy of Trust deed No. 17/2024 of Cheppad SRO. Ext. A2 to A4 are the copies of Settlement deed Nos. 1669/22, 298/24 and 299/24 of Cheppad SRO. Ext. A5 is the copy of FIR in crime No. 574/2024 of Kareelakulangara Police station. Ext. C1 commission report has identified plaint schedule property with *Meda, Nalukett and Nelpura*. Ext. C1 would also show that renovation works are going on in the *Nalukett*. Ext. B1 is the copy of Deed of amendment of trust. Ext. B2 is copy of Trust deed No. 2/2007 of Cheppad SRO. Ext. B3 is the judgment of Hon'ble High Court of Kerala in WP(C) No. 31247 dated 27.08.2025.

**9.** It is not in dispute that defendant is the son of the Trustee of the plaintiff trust. Ext. B1 would show that the said deed is a deed of amendment of trust deed No. 2/2007. Father of defendant is a trustee to Ext. B2 deed No. 2/2007, which was formed inter alia for preserving the heritage of the family, renovate and protect the existing heritage buildings. Ext. B1 trust deed amendment to clause 17 would show that the original authors of the trust and their legal heirs are the beneficiaries of the trust. Whether defendant, being the son of the trustee of plaintiff trust, is a beneficiary of the trust is a matter of evidence which has to be adjudicated after trial. Whether plaintiffs are in exclusive possession over plaint schedule properties and buildings therein is also a matter to be adjudicated after trial. Whether Ext. A1 deed of amendment is binding on defendant is also a matter of evidence. I am unable to accept the case of plaintiffs that defendant has no right over plaint schedule property being Alummoottil Tharavadu Trust property and is attempting to trespass therein.

**10.** Accordingly, I am satisfied that plaintiffs have failed to establish a prima facie case. No irreparable or serious injury will be caused to the petitioners if the above petition is not allowed. However, if the petition is allowed, defendant will be denuded from enjoying his right over plaint schedule property. Balance of convenience is not in favour of the petitioners. Accordingly I find no merit in the above petition.

**In the result, petition is dismissed. No order as to costs.**
