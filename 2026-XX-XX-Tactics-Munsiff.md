## 6. Anticipated defence footholds (have the answer ready)

- **Clause 14 / Clause 7 vs Section 11 ITA.** Defence: the Deed reserves amendment/management to authors and trustees, not beneficiaries. Answer: changing the *beneficiary class* is distinct from changing trustees, and that is what triggers the Section 11 all-beneficiaries-consent requirement. Do not plead the consent requirement as self-evident.
- **Clause 15 vs the C.K. Geeta point.** Defence: Geeta's signing was deed-sanctioned (legal heirs "shall be admitted"). Answer: admission still needs a shown instrument (none produced), and a deceased author cannot consent. Keep the point at "no instrument admitting her shown," **not** "she had no right."
- **Lineage is a two-step chain.** They admit "son of Radhakrishnan"; the Deed supplies "Radhakrishnan is a son of Chellamma." Do **not** plead that they admitted he is "Chellamma's grandson." Quote the beneficiary class exactly from the 2007 Deed recital (p.3): "members of the Alumoottil family who are the descendants of Alumoottil Chellamma Channathy."
- **Rejoinder misquotes.** The 13/03/2026 rejoinder put in quotation marks phrases not in the Reply ("zero funding," "no accounts," "no vouchers or records," "absolute possession," "immediate control"). Use only verbatim Reply wording going forward; be ready if the defence says we misquote their affidavit.
